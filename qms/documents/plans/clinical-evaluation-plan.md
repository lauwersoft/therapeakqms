# Clinical Evaluation Plan

## 1. Purpose

This plan defines the clinical evaluation strategy for the Therapeak AI therapy platform, a Class IIa medical device software (MDSW) under EU MDR 2017/745. The clinical evaluation shall demonstrate sufficient clinical evidence to confirm that the device achieves its intended purpose, that the benefits outweigh the residual risks, and that the device meets the relevant General Safety and Performance Requirements (GSPRs) of Annex I.

The clinical evaluation follows the MDCG 2020-1 guidance pathway for medical device software: establishing a valid clinical association between the device's output and the target clinical condition, demonstrating technical performance (the device produces accurate/reliable outputs), and demonstrating clinical performance (those outputs lead to clinically meaningful benefits for patients).

## 2. Scope

This plan covers the clinical evaluation of Therapeak version 1.0 (`DEVICE_MODE=medical`), an AI-powered conversational therapy platform intended to provide patient-specific supportive conversational guidance to help adults (19+) self-manage mild to moderate mental health symptoms at home.

### 2.1 Device Description

- **Intended purpose:** Therapeak provides patient-specific supportive conversational guidance intended to help users self-manage mild to moderate mental health symptoms at home.
- **Target conditions:** Mild to moderate anxiety disorders, depression, obsessive-compulsive disorders, trauma/stress-related disorders, and disorders related to impulse control.
- **Target population:** Adults aged 19 and older.
- **Classification:** Class IIa under MDR Annex VIII, Rule 11.
- **IMDRF category:** Informs clinical management.
- **Clinical claims:** The device supports understanding and self-monitoring of mental health symptoms. It does NOT diagnose, triage, recommend specific clinical interventions, or determine medication/treatment changes.

### 2.2 MDCG 2020-1 Pathway

For MDSW, clinical evidence is established through a three-step approach:

1. **Valid clinical association** -- Published scientific evidence demonstrates that the type of output generated by the software (conversational therapeutic guidance based on CBT/evidence-based principles) is associated with clinical benefit for the target conditions.
2. **Technical performance** -- Verification and validation evidence demonstrates that the software reliably and accurately generates the intended outputs (appropriate therapeutic conversations, session summaries, mood tracking).
3. **Clinical performance** -- Evidence that the device's outputs lead to clinically meaningful outcomes in the intended patient population (symptom improvement, user satisfaction, safety).

## 3. Literature Search Strategy

### 3.1 Databases

| Database | Rationale |
|---|---|
| PubMed/MEDLINE | Primary biomedical literature database |
| Cochrane Library | Systematic reviews and meta-analyses |
| PsycINFO | Psychology-specific literature |
| IEEE Xplore | Technical AI/ML in healthcare literature |
| Google Scholar | Supplementary search for grey literature |

### 3.2 Search Terms

The following search term combinations shall be used:

**Primary search (valid clinical association):**
- ("AI therapy" OR "AI chatbot" OR "conversational AI" OR "AI-based mental health" OR "digital mental health" OR "chatbot therapy") AND ("depression" OR "anxiety" OR "mental health" OR "psychological wellbeing")
- ("digital therapeutic" OR "digital intervention" OR "mHealth") AND ("CBT" OR "cognitive behavioral therapy") AND ("efficacy" OR "effectiveness" OR "RCT")

**Secondary search (equivalent devices):**
- ("Limbic" OR "Wysa" OR "Woebot" OR "Therabot" OR "SilverCloud") AND ("clinical" OR "efficacy" OR "safety" OR "CE marking")

**Tertiary search (safety):**
- ("AI therapy" OR "chatbot therapy" OR "digital mental health") AND ("safety" OR "adverse event" OR "harm" OR "risk")

### 3.3 Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Published 2017 or later (to align with MDR timeframe and current AI technology)
- RCTs, controlled trials, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, observational studies
- Adult populations (18+)
- AI-based or digital conversational interventions for mental health
- English language (or with English abstract)

**Exclusion criteria:**
- Pediatric-only populations
- Non-conversational digital interventions (e.g., pure app-based mood tracking without conversational component)
- Case reports and opinion pieces (unless relevant to safety)
- Studies of severe/complex mental health conditions (psychosis, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia)

### 3.4 Literature Appraisal

Each identified study shall be appraised for:
- Methodological quality (using GRADE framework for RCTs, AMSTAR 2 for systematic reviews)
- Relevance to the intended purpose and target population
- Applicability to the MDCG 2020-1 evidence pathway (valid clinical association, technical performance, or clinical performance)

## 4. Equivalence Assessment

### 4.1 Primary Comparator: Limbic Access

Limbic Access is identified as the primary equivalent device comparator because it is the only known AI mental health chatbot with Class IIa CE marking under EU MDR 2017/745.

| Equivalence Criterion | Limbic Access | Therapeak | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Clinical:** Intended purpose | AI-based mental health support, IAPT pathway screening/support | AI-based conversational therapy for mild-moderate mental health symptoms | Similar -- both provide AI-driven mental health support |
| **Clinical:** Target population | Adults with common mental health conditions | Adults 19+ with mild-moderate mental health conditions | Similar |
| **Clinical:** Target conditions | Anxiety, depression, common mental health disorders | Anxiety, depression, OCD, trauma/stress disorders, impulse control | Overlapping -- Therapeak has broader scope |
| **Technical:** Technology | AI/NLP-based chatbot software | AI/LLM-based chatbot software (Claude via OpenRouter) | Similar -- both use AI for conversational interaction |
| **Technical:** Platform | Web-based and integrated into NHS systems | Web-based (responsive web app) | Similar |
| **Biological:** Materials in contact | N/A (software only) | N/A (software only) | Equivalent |

### 4.2 Equivalence Conclusion

Full equivalence cannot be claimed due to differences in AI architecture (Limbic uses proprietary NLP; Therapeak uses third-party LLMs), clinical scope, and deployment context. However, Limbic serves as a valuable regulatory precedent and clinical comparator for the Class IIa AI mental health chatbot category. Clinical evidence for Therapeak shall therefore rely on a combination of equivalent device data, published literature, and own clinical data.

## 5. Clinical Data

### 5.1 Published Clinical Evidence (Valid Clinical Association)

The following key studies establish the valid clinical association between AI-based conversational therapy and clinical benefit in the target conditions:

**Randomized Controlled Trials:**

| Study | N | Duration | Condition | Effect Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Therabot (NEJM AI, 2025) | 210 | 8 weeks | Depression, anxiety | Depression d=0.85, anxiety d=0.79 | First RCT for fully generative AI therapy chatbot. Academic (Dartmouth), no commercial conflict. |
| Woebot (JMIR, 2017) | 70 | 2 weeks | Depression | d=0.44 | Early evidence. Co-author conflict of interest (Woebot founder). |
| Friend chatbot (BMC Psychology, 2025) | 104 | 8 weeks | Anxiety (women in war zones) | d=0.56-0.61 | Demonstrates efficacy in high-stress population. |

**Meta-Analyses:**

| Study | k (RCTs) | N | Key Findings |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feng, Tian et al. (JMIR, 2025) | 31 | 29,637 | Depression SMD=-0.43, anxiety SMD=-0.37, stress SMD=-0.41 |
| Linardon et al. (World Psychiatry, 2024) | 176 | -- | Apps with chatbot technology: depression g=0.53 |
| Zhu et al. (JMIR, 2025) | 14 | 6,314 | Mental health overall ES=0.30 |
| Zhong et al. (J Affective Disorders, 2024) | 18 | 3,477 | Depression g=-0.26, anxiety g=-0.19 |

**Context:** SSRI antidepressants achieve effect sizes of d=0.24-0.31. AI chatbot therapy effects (d=0.26-0.85 for depression) are comparable to or exceed pharmacological treatment, supporting a strong valid clinical association.

### 5.2 Own Pre-Market Clinical Data

Therapeak has collected pre-market experience data from its wellness product (not CE marked), which provides supporting evidence:

- **Sample:** 117 subscribers who joined August-September 2025, completed 14 or more sessions and 6 or more self-assessments.
- **Outcome:** 45% of users report feeling better (comparing earliest vs. most recent mood ratings).
- **Estimated effect size:** d approximately 0.5-0.7 (estimate based on comparison with similar published studies).
- **Limitation:** Based on self-reported mood assessments, not standardized clinical scales (PHQ-9, GAD-7). The medical device version will incorporate validated outcome measures.

### 5.3 Evidence Synthesis

The combined evidence demonstrates:

1. **Valid clinical association (strong):** Multiple RCTs and meta-analyses confirm that AI-based conversational therapy produces clinically meaningful improvements in depression and anxiety symptoms, with effect sizes comparable to or exceeding established pharmacological treatments.
2. **Technical performance:** To be demonstrated through software verification and validation activities per [[PLN-005]], including prompt testing, session quality monitoring, and output quality assessment.
3. **Clinical performance (preliminary):** Own pre-market data from 117 users shows preliminary clinical benefit consistent with published literature. Formal clinical performance evaluation will be conducted as part of PMCF activities per [[PLN-003]].

## 6. Benefit-Risk Determination

### 6.1 Clinical Benefits

- Accessible, affordable mental health support available 24/7 from any location
- Reduction in anxiety and depression symptoms (supported by literature effect sizes d=0.26-0.85)
- Support for self-management of mild to moderate mental health conditions
- Complementary to traditional therapy (can be used alongside professional treatment)
- Reduced barrier to mental health support (no waitlists, no stigma of in-person visits)

### 6.2 Residual Risks

- User may receive therapeutically suboptimal AI output (mitigated by prompt engineering, session monitoring, quality flags)
- User may misinterpret AI output as professional diagnosis (mitigated by disclaimers, IFU)
- User in crisis may rely on device instead of seeking emergency help (mitigated by crisis messaging, homepage disclaimer, contraindication statement)
- Data privacy risks from health data processing (mitigated by encryption, access controls, GDPR compliance)

### 6.3 Benefit-Risk Conclusion

The clinical evidence supports a favorable benefit-risk profile. The demonstrated clinical benefits (effect sizes comparable to antidepressants, consistent across multiple independent studies) outweigh the residual risks, which are mitigated through design controls, safety messaging, and post-market surveillance. The full benefit-risk analysis is documented in [[RA-001]].
