# Management Review Procedure

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the process for conducting management reviews of the Quality Management System to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness, and to identify opportunities for improvement.

**Related documents:** [[QM-001]] Quality Manual, [[FM-009]] Management Review Form, [[SOP-005]] Internal Audit Procedure

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to the periodic review of the entire Therapeak QMS, including all processes, performance data, and regulatory compliance matters described in [[QM-001]].

## 3. Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Sarp Derinsu (Management) | Conducts management review, makes decisions, documents outcomes |
| Suzan Slijpen (Consultant) | Provides regulatory input, reviews draft outcomes, advises on compliance matters |

### 3.1 One-Person Company Context

As Therapeak is a one-person company, the management review is conducted by Sarp Derinsu as both the sole operator and top management. The review is a structured, documented self-assessment of QMS performance against defined inputs. Suzan Slijpen participates as regulatory advisor where needed. All decisions and outcomes are documented using [[FM-009]] to ensure objective evidence of the review.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Review Frequency

Management reviews are conducted **every 6 months** (semi-annually). Minimum schedule:

| Review | Target Period |
|--------|--------------|
| H1 review | March/April |
| H2 review | September/October |

Additional reviews may be triggered by:
- Major nonconformities from internal or external audits
- Significant regulatory changes affecting the QMS
- Serious incidents or field safety corrective actions
- Significant changes to the organization or product

### 4.2 Preparation

Before each management review, Sarp shall collect and compile data for all review inputs (Section 4.3). This includes:

1. Gathering records from the previous 6-month period
2. Reviewing open action items from the previous management review
3. Compiling data into the [[FM-009]] Management Review Form
4. Consulting with Suzan on regulatory developments

Allow at least 1 week for preparation before the scheduled review date.

### 4.3 Review Inputs

The following inputs shall be reviewed at each management review, as required by [Clause 5.6.2](/references/iso-13485#clause-5-6-2):

| # | Input | Data Source |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Results of internal and external audits | [[SOP-005]] audit reports |
| 2 | Customer feedback and complaints | [[SOP-004]] complaint records, user contact messages |
| 3 | CAPA status and effectiveness | [[SOP-003]] CAPA records |
| 4 | Post-market surveillance data | [[SOP-009]] PMS reports, user feedback trends |
| 5 | Process performance and product conformity | Session quality monitoring data, system uptime, error rates |
| 6 | Status of preventive and corrective actions | Open CAPA and action item tracking |
| 7 | Follow-up actions from previous reviews | Previous [[FM-009]] records |
| 8 | Changes that could affect the QMS | Regulatory changes, product changes, organizational changes |
| 9 | Recommendations for improvement | Observations from audits, user feedback, self-assessment |
| 10 | New or revised regulatory requirements | MDR updates, MDCG guidance, harmonized standards |
| 11 | Training and competency status | [[SOP-010]] training records |
| 12 | Supplier performance | [[SOP-008]] supplier evaluation data |
| 13 | Risk management review | Risk management file status, new or changed risks |

### 4.4 Conducting the Review

Sarp shall:

1. Review each input systematically using [[FM-009]]
2. Assess whether the QMS remains suitable, adequate, and effective
3. Evaluate the quality policy and quality objectives for continued relevance
4. Identify areas where improvement is needed
5. Make decisions on actions required (see Section 4.5)
6. Document all findings, decisions, and action items in [[FM-009]]

Where regulatory expertise is needed, Sarp consults Suzan during or after the review.

### 4.5 Review Outputs

The management review shall produce documented decisions and actions related to, as required by [Clause 5.6.3](/references/iso-13485#clause-5-6-3):

| # | Output Category | Examples |
|---|----------------|----------|
| 1 | Improvement actions | Process changes, additional monitoring, new tools |
| 2 | Changes needed to the QMS | New or revised SOPs, updated forms, policy changes |
| 3 | Resource needs | Training, consultant support, infrastructure, software tools |
| 4 | Quality policy and objective updates | Revised targets, new objectives for the next period |
| 5 | Regulatory compliance actions | Responses to new guidance, preparation for NB audits |

Each action item shall include:
- Description of the action
- Responsible person (typically Sarp, or Suzan for regulatory tasks)
- Target completion date
- Priority (high, medium, low)

### 4.6 Communication and Follow-Up

1. Completed [[FM-009]] is stored as a controlled record per [[SOP-001]]
2. Action items are tracked and their status is reviewed at the next management review
3. If the review identifies a need for CAPA, initiate per [[SOP-003]]
4. Significant decisions are communicated to Suzan as applicable
