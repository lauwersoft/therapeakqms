# Training and Competency Procedure

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines how Therapeak B.V. identifies training needs, provides training, and verifies competency for all personnel involved in QMS-related activities. It ensures that everyone performing work affecting product quality and safety has the necessary education, training, skills, and experience.

**Related documents:** [[FM-006]] Training Record Form, [[LOG-001]] Training Log

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to:
- Sarp Derinsu (sole operator — software development, quality management, regulatory affairs, customer support, PMS, vigilance)
- Nisan Derinsu (director — emergency vigilance backup)
- Suzan Slijpen (external regulatory consultant — advisory role)
- Any future personnel who perform QMS-related activities

## 3. Responsibilities

| Role | Person | Responsibility |
|------|--------|---------------|
| Quality Manager | Sarp Derinsu | Identifies training needs, plans and delivers training, maintains training records, assesses competency |
| Director (Emergency Backup) | Nisan Derinsu | Completes required vigilance training, maintains competency for emergency backup role |
| Regulatory Consultant | Suzan Slijpen | Provides QMS and regulatory training to Sarp; training content does not require formal records within this QMS |

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Training Needs Identification

Training needs are identified based on the following areas:

| Training Area | Applicable To | Rationale |
|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| QMS processes | Sarp Derinsu | Must understand and be able to execute all QMS procedures |
| Regulatory requirements (EU MDR, ISO 13485) | Sarp Derinsu | Must understand applicable regulatory framework |
| Software development (IEC 62304 principles) | Sarp Derinsu | Must follow software lifecycle procedures |
| Clinical aspects (intended purpose, contraindications, risk) | Sarp Derinsu | Must understand the clinical context of the device |
| Emergency vigilance reporting | Nisan Derinsu | Must be able to identify and escalate serious incidents if Sarp is unavailable |

Training needs are reviewed:
- At least annually during management review
- When new procedures are introduced or significantly revised
- When new personnel are onboarded
- When nonconformities or CAPAs reveal competency gaps

### 4.2 Training Methods

Given that Therapeak is a one-person company, training is delivered through practical, proportionate methods:

| Method | Description |
|--------|------------|
| External consultant training | QMS overview and regulatory guidance provided by Suzan Slijpen |
| Self-directed study | Reading of ISO 13485, EU MDR, MDCG guidance documents, IEC 62304, and other applicable standards |
| On-the-job learning | Practical application of QMS procedures in day-to-day operations |
| Online courses and webinars | Industry training on regulatory affairs, quality management, and clinical evaluation |
| One-on-one briefing | For Nisan: targeted training on vigilance basics delivered by Sarp |

### 4.4 Training Delivery and Recording

1. All training activities are recorded using [[FM-006]] Training Record Form
2. Each record includes: trainee name, training topic, date, training method, trainer (if applicable), and competency assessment outcome
3. Completed training records are maintained in [[LOG-001]] Training Log
4. The Training Log provides a summary view of all personnel and their training status

### 4.5 Competency Assessment

Competency is assessed through:

| Assessment Method | When Used |
|-------------------|-----------|
| Demonstrated execution of procedures | Ongoing — competency is confirmed when QMS procedures are followed correctly (e.g., complaints handled per procedure, documents controlled per [[SOP-001]], CAPAs completed per [[SOP-003]]) |
| Review during internal audit | Annual internal audit verifies that procedures are being followed, which confirms competency |
| Review during management review | Training effectiveness is a standing agenda item |
| Practical demonstration | For Nisan: walkthrough of emergency vigilance scenario with Sarp |

If a competency gap is identified (e.g., through audit findings, CAPAs, or procedure deviations), additional training is provided and the effectiveness is re-assessed.

### 4.6 New Personnel Onboarding

If Therapeak hires additional personnel in the future:

1. Training needs are identified based on the person's role and QMS-related responsibilities
2. Required training must be completed **before** the person independently performs QMS-related activities
3. Training is delivered using the methods defined in Section 4.2
4. Competency is assessed and recorded before independent work begins
5. The Training Log [[LOG-001]] is updated accordingly

### 4.7 Ongoing Training

Training is not a one-time activity. Ongoing training is triggered by:
- Changes to QMS procedures
- New regulatory requirements or guidance
- Findings from audits, CAPAs, or post-market surveillance
- Changes to the device or its intended purpose
- Annual refresher review during management review
