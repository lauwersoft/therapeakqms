# Internal Audit Procedure

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the process for planning, conducting, and following up on internal audits of the Quality Management System to verify conformity with ISO 13485:2016, EU MDR 2017/745, and Therapeak's own QMS requirements.

**Related documents:** [[SOP-001]] Document Control, [[SOP-003]] CAPA Procedure, [[FM-008]] Internal Audit Checklist, [[FM-009]] Management Review Form

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to all internal audits of the Therapeak QMS, covering all processes, departments, and activities described in [[QM-001]] Quality Manual.

## 3. Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Sarp Derinsu (Management) | Approves annual audit schedule, reviews audit findings, ensures resources for corrective actions |
| External Auditor (jurist) | Conducts the audit independently, prepares audit report, classifies findings |
| Suzan Slijpen (Consultant) | Coordinates auditor engagement, supports audit planning, advises on findings |

### 3.1 Auditor Independence

As Therapeak is a one-person company, independence cannot be achieved with internal resources. All internal audits are conducted by an external auditor — a qualified jurist engaged through Suzan Slijpen (Pander Consultancy). This ensures the auditor has no direct involvement in the activities being audited, as required by [Clause 8.2.4](/references/iso-13485#clause-8-2-4).

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Audit Planning

Internal audits are conducted **annually**. Sarp shall:

1. Define the audit schedule at the beginning of each calendar year (or upon QMS establishment)
2. Ensure the audit covers all QMS processes within a 12-month cycle
3. Prioritize areas based on:
   - Previous audit findings and open corrective actions
   - Process changes since the last audit
   - Complaint and CAPA trends
   - Regulatory changes
   - Risk to product safety and performance
4. Communicate the audit scope and schedule to Suzan, who coordinates with the external auditor

The first internal audit is scheduled for **March 31, 2026**, prior to the Notified Body engagement on April 7, 2026.

### 4.2 Audit Preparation

The external auditor shall:

1. Receive access to the Therapeak QMS documentation
2. Review relevant QMS documents, including:
   - [[QM-001]] Quality Manual
   - Applicable SOPs, plans, and records
   - Previous audit reports and open findings (if any)
3. Prepare an audit plan specifying:
   - Audit date(s) and estimated duration
   - Processes and clauses to be audited
   - Audit criteria (ISO 13485 clauses, MDR articles, internal procedures)
4. Prepare or use [[FM-008]] Internal Audit Checklist

Sarp shall ensure all requested documentation and records are available to the auditor.

### 4.3 Audit Execution

The audit consists of:

1. **Opening meeting** — Auditor confirms scope, schedule, and methodology with Sarp
2. **Document review** — Auditor examines QMS documents, records, and evidence of implementation
3. **Interview** — Auditor interviews Sarp on process implementation, decision-making, and awareness of QMS requirements
4. **Objective evidence collection** — Auditor verifies that documented procedures are followed in practice by examining records, system logs, and work outputs
5. **Closing meeting** — Auditor presents preliminary findings to Sarp

Given the company size, audits are typically completed in one day.

### 4.4 Finding Classification

The auditor classifies each finding as:

| Classification | Definition | Response Required |
|---------------|------------|-------------------|
| Major nonconformity | Absence or total breakdown of a required process; direct risk to product safety or regulatory compliance | CAPA required within 30 days |
| Minor nonconformity | Isolated lapse or partial implementation of a required process; no direct safety impact | Corrective action within 60 days |
| Observation | Opportunity for improvement; no nonconformity but a weakness that could become one | Addressed at next management review |

### 4.5 Audit Report

The external auditor shall produce a written audit report within 10 business days of the audit, containing:

1. Audit scope, criteria, and date(s)
2. Processes and clauses audited
3. Summary of findings, classified per Section 4.4
4. Objective evidence supporting each finding
5. Positive observations (areas of good practice)
6. Overall audit conclusion

The report is delivered to Sarp and Suzan.

### 4.6 Corrective Actions

For each nonconformity identified:

1. Sarp reviews and accepts the findings
2. For major and minor nonconformities, a CAPA is initiated per [[SOP-003]]
3. Root cause analysis is performed
4. Corrective actions are defined with target completion dates
5. Actions are implemented and evidence is documented
6. Effectiveness of corrective actions is verified

Observations are logged and addressed during the next management review per [[SOP-006]].

### 4.7 Follow-Up and Closure

1. Sarp tracks all audit findings and corrective action status
2. Evidence of corrective action completion is documented
3. For major nonconformities, the external auditor may verify the effectiveness of corrective actions
4. Findings are closed only when corrective actions are implemented and verified as effective
5. Audit results and open findings are included as input to [[SOP-006]] Management Review

### 4.8 Audit Schedule

| Audit | Planned Date | Scope |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| First internal audit | March 31, 2026 | Full QMS (all processes) — pre-NB engagement |
| Subsequent audits | Annually (Q1) | Full QMS, with priority areas based on risk |

The schedule may be adjusted based on significant changes (e.g., major nonconformities, regulatory changes, significant design changes).
