# MDCG 2020-6 - Regulation (EU) 2017/745: Clinical Evidence Needed for Medical Devices Previously CE Marked Under Directives 93/42/EEC or 90/385/EEC — A Guide for Manufacturers and Notified Bodies

April 2020

This document has been endorsed by the Medical Device Coordination Group (MDCG) established by Article 103 of Regulation (EU) 2017/745. The MDCG is composed of representatives of all Member States and it is chaired by a representative of the European Commission. The document is not a European Commission document and it cannot be regarded as reflecting the official position of the European Commission. Any views expressed in this document are not legally binding and only the Court of Justice of the European Union can give binding interpretations of Union law.

## 1. Purpose

This guidance aims to assist manufacturers and notified bodies in determining the level of clinical evidence needed for medical devices that were previously CE marked under the Medical Devices Directive (93/42/EEC) or the Active Implantable Medical Devices Directive (90/385/EEC) and are now transitioning to the MDR.

## 2. Scope

This guidance applies to legacy devices — medical devices that already have clinical history under the MDD/AIMDD and need to demonstrate sufficient clinical evidence under the MDR's enhanced requirements.

## 3. Key Principles

### 3.1 Enhanced Clinical Evidence Requirements Under MDR

The MDR introduces more stringent clinical evidence requirements compared to the MDD. Legacy devices must now demonstrate:

- Clinical evidence that confirms the safety and performance of the device
- Updated benefit-risk assessment based on current state of the art
- Consideration of any post-market data collected during the device's market history

### 3.2 Gap Analysis

Manufacturers of legacy devices should perform a gap analysis to identify:

- Differences between the clinical evidence currently held and what is required under the MDR
- Areas where additional clinical investigations or studies may be needed
- Post-market clinical follow-up data that can supplement existing evidence

### 3.3 Use of Existing Clinical Data

Existing clinical data from the MDD era may be used to support MDR compliance, provided:

- The data was collected in accordance with good clinical practice
- The data is still relevant and representative of the current device
- Any gaps are addressed through supplementary evidence

## 4. Relevance to New Devices

While primarily targeted at legacy devices, the principles in this guidance regarding sufficient clinical evidence, gap analysis, and benefit-risk assessment methodology are also applicable to new devices entering the market under the MDR.
