# MDCG 2020-3 - Guidance on Significant Changes Regarding the Transitional Provision Under Article 120 of MDR

April 2020 (Revision 1, October 2021)

This document has been endorsed by the Medical Device Coordination Group (MDCG) established by Article 103 of Regulation (EU) 2017/745. The MDCG is composed of representatives of all Member States and it is chaired by a representative of the European Commission. The document is not a European Commission document and it cannot be regarded as reflecting the official position of the European Commission. Any views expressed in this document are not legally binding and only the Court of Justice of the European Union can give binding interpretations of Union law.

## 1. Purpose

This guidance document aims to assist manufacturers and notified bodies in determining what constitutes a "significant change" to a medical device. Under the MDR, certain changes to a device that has been certified under the MDD or AIMDD may trigger the requirement for a new conformity assessment under the MDR rather than continuing under the transitional provisions.

The concept of significant changes is also relevant for devices certified under the MDR, where changes may require notification to or additional assessment by the notified body.

## 2. Scope

This guidance applies to:

- Devices covered by certificates issued under the MDD (93/42/EEC) or AIMDD (90/385/EEC) that are subject to Article 120 transitional provisions
- Devices certified under the MDR where changes may affect the basis of the conformity assessment
- All risk classes of medical devices

## 3. Definition of Significant Change

A change is considered significant if it could affect:

1. **The safety or performance of the device** — any modification that has, or could have, an adverse effect on the safety or performance of the device
2. **The conditions upon which the conformity assessment was based** — any alteration that changes the basis on which the certificate was granted

The assessment of whether a change is significant must be performed on a case-by-case basis by the manufacturer, taking into account the potential impact of the change.

## 4. Categories of Changes

### 4.1 Changes to the Intended Purpose

Changes to the intended purpose are generally considered significant. This includes:

- New or modified indications
- New or modified target patient population
- New or modified intended users
- New or modified clinical benefits claimed
- New or modified contraindications
- Changes to the medical condition being addressed

### 4.2 Design Changes

Design changes should be evaluated for their impact on safety and performance:

- Changes to materials or components
- Changes to manufacturing processes
- Changes to the design or construction of the device
- Software changes (functionality, algorithms, architecture)
- Changes to accessories or other devices used in combination

### 4.3 Changes to Quality Management System

Certain QMS changes may be significant if they affect the conformity assessment:

- Changes to critical suppliers
- Changes to sterilisation processes
- Changes to manufacturing site
- Major changes to quality management procedures that affect product realisation

### 4.4 Changes Resulting from New Scientific/Technical Knowledge

Changes driven by new evidence may be significant:

- New clinical data indicating safety concerns
- New knowledge about materials or substances
- Post-market surveillance data indicating needed changes
- Regulatory changes requiring modifications

## 5. Assessment Criteria

### 5.1 General Principles

The following principles apply when assessing significance:

1. The assessment should consider the **totality** of the change, not individual elements in isolation
2. Multiple non-significant changes may, when considered together, constitute a significant change
3. The assessment should be documented and traceable
4. When in doubt, the manufacturer should consult the notified body

### 5.2 Questions to Consider

For each proposed change, the manufacturer should consider:

- Does the change affect the clinical evidence base?
- Does the change affect the risk-benefit profile?
- Does the change affect the design verification or validation?
- Does the change require new or updated testing?
- Does the change affect the labelling or instructions for use?
- Does the change affect the classification of the device?

## 6. Software-Specific Guidance

For software changes, the following factors should be considered:

- **New or modified algorithms** that affect the output or clinical decision
- **Changes to the software architecture** that affect data integrity, security, or reliability
- **Changes to the AI/ML model** that affect the clinical performance or safety of the device
- **New data sources or inputs** that change the basis of the device's function
- **Changes to the user interface** that could affect safe use

Software bug fixes and minor performance improvements are generally not considered significant, provided they do not change the intended function or introduce new risks.

## 7. Obligations

### 7.1 Manufacturer Obligations

- Maintain a change management process (per ISO 13485 Clause 7.3.9)
- Document the significance assessment for each change
- Notify the notified body of significant changes before implementation (where required)
- Update the technical documentation as appropriate
- Ensure traceability of changes

### 7.2 Notified Body Obligations

- Review and assess notified significant changes
- Evaluate whether the change affects the basis of the existing certificate
- Determine whether additional assessment activities are needed
- Issue supplementary certificates or updated certificates as appropriate

## 8. Documentation Requirements

The manufacturer should maintain documentation for each change including:

1. Description of the change
2. Rationale for the change
3. Significance assessment with justification
4. Impact analysis on safety, performance, and conformity assessment
5. Updated risk management documentation (if applicable)
6. Verification and validation activities performed
7. Updated technical documentation (if applicable)
8. Notification to notified body (if applicable) and their response
