# EU MDR Certification - An Overview

An overview of the EU MDR certification process and the requirements for submitting quality management system documentation and technical documentation.

The European Medical Device Regulation (EU MDR) 2017/745 is the regulatory framework that governs medical devices placed on the market in the European Union. EU MDR certification demonstrates that your medical device meets the essential safety and performance requirements necessary to bear the CE marking and be legally placed on the European market.

## Requirements

EU MDR certification requires comprehensive technical documentation that demonstrates your medical device meets the General Safety and Performance Requirements (GSPRs) outlined in Annex I of the regulation. Your submission must include both quality management system documentation and technical documentation covering all relevant work streams for your device.

Requirements placed on manufacturers concerning the technical documentation content are scattered throughout the EU MDR guidelines and thus can be challenging to comprehend and assemble. This includes the following non-exhaustive examples:

- The classification of the device (Article 51)
- Clinical evaluation (Article 61 & Annex XIV, Part A)
- Clinical investigation (Article 62)
- Post-market clinical follow-up (Annex XIV, Part B)
- Device description and specification (Annex II.1)
- Information provided by the manufacturer (Annex II.2)
- Design and manufacturing information (Annex II.3)
- General safety and performance requirements (Annex I, Annex II.4)
- Benefit-risk analysis and risk management (Annex II.5)
- Product verification and validation (Annex II.6)
- The summary of safety and clinical performance (Article 32)
- Post-market surveillance (Chapter VII, Annex III)
- Declaration of conformity (Annex IV)
- CE marking of conformity (Annex V)
- Information on the registration of devices (Article 29, Annex VI)

To further complicate matters, EU MDR Annex I, 17.2 places the following expectation on SaMD manufacturers:

> "For devices that incorporate software or for software that are devices in themselves, the software shall be developed and manufactured in accordance with the state of the art taking into account the principles of development life cycle, risk management, including information security, verification and validation."

This means manufacturers are responsible for defining and evidencing compliance with state-of-the-art standards relevant to the development, release and maintenance of their SaMD.

## Typical Certification Flow

The EU MDR certification process with Scarlet involves several key stages:

### Pre-submission

1. Device scope and classification determination
2. Quality management system establishment and ISO 13485 certification
3. Technical documentation preparation
4. Pre-application structured dialogues (optional but recommended)

### Submission and Assessment

1. Document submission and application review
2. Quality management system audits (Stage 1 and Stage 2)
3. Technical documentation assessment
4. Query response and resolution
5. Final review and decision making (FRDM)

### Post-certification

1. Surveillance audits (conducted at 12-month intervals)
2. Unannounced audits (at least once every 5 years, or every 3 years for Class III devices)
3. Post-market surveillance and clinical follow-up
4. Recertification audit (before certificate expiry)
