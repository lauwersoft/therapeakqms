# Quality Management for EU MDR

EU MDR Article 10(9) requires manufacturers to establish and maintain a quality management system (QMS) that integrates with technical documentation and demonstrates ongoing compliance throughout the device lifecycle.

This article outlines the general expectations for quality management system (QMS) documentation that manufacturers should prepare when applying for initial EU MDR certification through Scarlet. These materials demonstrate compliance with EU MDR requirements related to quality assurance, safety, and regulatory readiness. EU MDR quality management requirements are closely aligned with ISO 13485:2016, but with important regulatory differences including integration with technical documentation, PRRC requirements, and enhanced post-market surveillance obligations.

While the EU MDR defines the required outcomes, it is the manufacturer's responsibility to establish, document, implement, and maintain appropriate processes and procedures that reflect their specific operations.

## QMS Requirements

> **Important Disclaimer:** This is not an exhaustive list. Requirements may vary depending on the organisation's size, the nature of the medical device, and its risk classification.

The EU MDR (2017/745), particularly Annex IX (Chapter I) and Article 10, sets out general QMS requirements for manufacturers. The table below aligns core MDR expectations with typical documentation:

| EU MDR Requirement | Supporting Documentation |
|---|---|
| General safety and performance requirements (GSPRs) | Procedure for demonstrating compliance with GSPRs and maintaining a checklist per Annex I. |
| Regulatory compliance function | Proof of Person Responsible for Regulatory Compliance (PRRC), including evidence of formal qualification and/or CV, and job role description. |
| Clinical evaluation & PMS | Integration of clinical evaluation and PMS within the QMS; linkage to clinical and post-market procedures. |
| Risk-benefit analysis | Alignment of risk management with benefit-risk evaluations; procedures referencing ISO 14971. |
| UDI & labelling controls | Labelling control procedures and Unique Device Identification (UDI) implementation. |
| Market surveillance strategy | PMS Plan, Periodic Safety Update Report (PSUR), and PMCF Plan, as applicable. |
| Vigilance and incident reporting | Process for handling serious incidents, field safety corrective actions (FSCA), and regulatory notifications. |
| Change management | Procedure for evaluating and notifying significant changes to the QMS, device, or regulatory strategy. |
| Post-market surveillance | Evidence of feedback loops from market data into risk, design, and production controls. |
| Supplier and subcontractor oversight | Contracts and agreements with critical suppliers, including quality agreements and audit records. |
| Administrative provisions | Procedure defining document retention periods, including secure storage, accessibility, and responsibility for record maintenance. |
| Marketing and claims compliance | Process to ensure that advertising and promotional materials are accurate, not misleading, and consistent with approved indications and labelling. |

## Additional Guidance for Applicants

Manufacturers are encouraged to:

- Align QMS procedures with ISO 13485 clauses and MDR expectations to avoid duplication and ensure coherence.
- Conduct a gap assessment prior to submission to identify missing procedures, misalignments, or outdated practices.
- Provide traceability between procedures, records, and the device's lifecycle -- this helps demonstrate control over design, production, post-market phases, and feedback.
- Ensure objective evidence is available for all applicable requirements. Auditors must be able to clearly see what was done, by whom, when, and in what sequence. This includes completed forms, logs, training records, meeting minutes, validation reports, audit trails, etc. It is essential to understand that it is not enough to describe a process -- it must be implemented, documented, and demonstrable.
