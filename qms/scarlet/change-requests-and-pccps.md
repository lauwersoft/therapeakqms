# Change Requests and Pre-determined Change Control Plans (PCCPs)

## Change Requests

As a manufacturer, you're responsible for keeping your certification up to date so you need to know when to notify us about changes to your device, device range and/or QMS.

The short answer: If a change is substantial, you must notify us and wait for approval before implementing it (i.e. placing on the market). Not every change request will lead to a full conformity assessment -- minor changes may require minimal action.

If you'd like to implement substantial changes without notifying us in advance, you must have a Pre-determined Change Control Plan (PCCP) submitted and approved by Scarlet.

It's up to you to determine if a change is substantial based on the regulations. We will review your decision-making during audits, ensuring you have a robust process for assessing changes internally.

### How to Submit a Change Request

Please inform your Account Manager that you would like to submit a change request. Your AM will set up the change request form in your Scarlet workspace. You will complete the form and upload relevant documents.

### What Happens After You Submit a Change Request?

We begin the review. You must wait to implement the change until we approve it. Depending on the impact, the approval process may require no action, a certificate supplement or additional assessment.

### What Is a Substantial Change?

For information on utilising pre-determined Change Control Plans (PCCP) to reduce change approvals, please see the PCCP section below.

## Pre-determined Change Control Plans (PCCP)

Predetermined change control plans (PCCPs) are dawning as a new option for manufacturers, under MDR, to enact pre-accepted changes to their SaMD in the post-market phase.

While submission of a PCCP is not currently mandated within the UK or EU for technical file submissions, Scarlet recognises the clear international regulatory alignment on the importance and anticipated benefits of PCCPs.

Accordingly, Scarlet enables and encourages manufacturers to submit PCCPs so that planned AI/ML-driven modifications can be managed in a predictable, structured, and regulatory-compliant manner throughout the product lifecycle.

### What Is a PCCP?

A PCCP is a plan, assessed and approved during initial conformity assessment or surveillance, that outlines specific changes that the manufacturer intends to make to the device in the post-market phase.

These plans detail:

- Which changes a manufacturer plans to make to the device(s)
- How they will develop, validate, and implement those changes
- How they will assess, monitor, and control their impact on device safety and performance

For a PCCP to be approved, manufacturers must submit:

- Clear, bounded descriptions of planned changes
- Robust, predefined protocols covering data, retraining, performance evaluation, and deployment
- A robust, ISO 14971-aligned risk assessment
- Evidence that changes remain within the intended purpose
- Traceability and integration into QMS and technical documentation

This ensures:

- Safe, predictable AI/ML iteration
- Reduced regulatory friction
- Transparent lifecycle management
- Compliance with FDA, UK MDR, and IMDRF expectations
- Efficient and consistent Scarlet review

A PCCP can be submitted with the technical file submission at initial conformity assessment or during a surveillance assessment.

A separate PCCP must be submitted for each device within the QMS certificate, as the PCCP is part of a device's technical file.

### PCCP Purpose

A PCCP enables manufacturers to pre-define a bounded set of changes to an AI/ML software as a medical device (SaMD) and the protocols, evidence and risk controls that will govern these changes. Once approved, these changes may be implemented without requiring separate, repeated change submissions, provided the manufacturer adheres to the authorised plan.

Across the medical device community, PCCPs are viewed as helpful with:

- Aligning regulatory processes with the rapid and ongoing approach to change management in AI/ML devices
- Maintaining high regulatory standards for safety, performance and benefit-risk across iterative updates
- Managing risks in a timely and ongoing fashion through monitoring, maintenance, and/or improving device performance

PCCP can help achieve this by requiring transparent, validated and risk-managed software evolution through providing clear verification, validation and update procedures.

### Scope for Changes Eligible for PCCP

A PCCP may only include substantial changes that remain within the device's intended purpose. These are changes that may significantly affect performance, inputs, or model output, but fundamentally do not alter the intended purpose of the device.

**Example of a change that fits PCCP scope:** An AI dermatology classifier of melanoma expands its validated performance to include darker skin tones within the already approved adult population, supported by new dataset training and performance validation. The intended purpose (aid in identifying suspicious lesions in adults) is unchanged.

**Example of a change that does not fit PCCP scope:** An AI dermatology classifier of melanoma is updated to support the identification of eczema, representing a new clinical condition not covered in the intended purpose. This alters the device's intended purpose and therefore cannot be included in a PCCP; it requires a change request post-certification.

### What to Expect from Scarlet During Review

We will:

- Assess completeness of the PCCP and identify gaps or ambiguous boundaries
- Classify each planned change as:
  - Non-substantial
  - Substantial but out of scope
  - Substantial and approvable within PCCP
- Confirm the PCCP's coherence with intended purpose and technical documentation
- Provide formal approval or findings requiring modification of the plan

### What Happens After PCCP Is Approved

Once a PCCP has been approved, the manufacturer should formally document the specific version of the PCCP authorised by Scarlet to ensure full traceability within the technical documentation and QMS records. For any substantial changes that fall within the boundaries of the approved PCCP, a separate change request to Scarlet is not required, as long as the manufacturer implements the modification strictly in accordance with the pre-defined and approved Change Protocol. Customers shall update Scarlet that a substantial change has occurred, in accordance with the approved PCCP, within 10 working days of implementation of the change.

This exemption applies only to the planned substantial changes explicitly included within the approved PCCP. Any substantial changes that are outside those defined boundaries require notification to Scarlet through a change request, prior to implementation of the change. Additionally, any substantial deviation from the approved PCCP, including failure to follow the agreed data, retraining, performance, or deployment procedures, must be documented by the manufacturer and notified to Scarlet without delay, in accordance with regulatory expectations. Deviations will be assessed to determine whether further change assessment activities are required.

During routine surveillance assessments, Scarlet will review the substantial changes implemented under the approved PCCP to confirm they align with the agreed change protocols. Manufacturers must therefore ensure that all PCCP-implemented changes are clearly documented within the technical file, with explicit referencing to each implemented change back to the corresponding section of the PCCP. Documentation should be easy to locate, clearly version-controlled, and sufficiently detailed to demonstrate compliance with the approved Change Protocol, supporting efficient and transparent surveillance review.
