# Usability Engineering Reports

> Documentation that summarises the outcomes of usability engineering evaluation activities to obtain objective evidence that the user interface can be used safely.

Usability engineering evaluation reports document the outcomes of evaluation activities performed on medical device software. These reports provide evidence that the user interface can be used safely, focusing on evaluating hazard-related use scenarios, use errors and related risk control measures.

The outcomes of formative evaluation activities can help manufacturers identify and address usability issues early in the development process, reducing the likelihood of safety issues in the final product.

Regulatory expectations under IEC 62366-1 and EU MDR require manufacturers to document the outcomes of summative evaluation activities, including any new risk elements discovered and the analysis of required design improvements. Critically, the summative evaluation report must indicate whether use errors and hazard-related use scenarios have been adequately evaluated, provide evidence that any residual risk remains acceptable, and confirm that the user interface has been deemed safe to use. Additionally, granular traceability must be provided to demonstrate how each user error and relevant risk control measures have been adequately addressed through the evaluation activities.

See the [deep dive section](#deep-dive) for more details on the expected content of these usability engineering reports for both summative and formative evaluation phases.

## Minimum Requirements

When submitting your technical documentation, the following are the minimum requirements for this topic:

- **Summative evaluation reports**: For each planned activity, the documentation must report the personnel who conducted it, whether it was conducted according to plan (including any deviations), the results and conclusions, any new risk elements discovered, and provide concluding remarks that analyse design improvements, demonstrate acceptable residual risk, and confirm safe use of the user interface.
- **Summative evaluation conclusions**: The documentation must provide concluding statements to indicate if use errors and relevant risk controls have been adequately evaluated, and the medical device's user interface(s) have been deemed safe to use.
- **Traceability**: The documentation must provide traceability of usability-related use requirements and risk elements to summative evaluation activities, and traceability of activities to activity reports.

> **Note:** Evidence of formative evaluation is not specifically required for certification but is strongly recommended as best practice. Formative evaluation activities conducted throughout the development lifecycle can help identify and address usability issues early, reducing the likelihood of safety issues in the final product.

## Common Pitfalls

The following are common queries that are raised by Scarlet during assessment:

- **Incomplete activity reports**: Reports that fail to document personnel who conducted activities, whether activities were conducted according to plan, deviations, results and conclusions, or new risk elements discovered.
- **Missing or inadequate concluding remarks**: Reports that fail to analyse user interface design and required improvements, do not provide evidence that residual risk remains acceptable, or fail to confirm that use errors and hazard-related use scenarios have been adequately evaluated and the interface is safe to use.
- **Insufficient traceability**: Documentation that lacks traceability of use errors and risk controls to evaluation activities, or traceability of planned activities to execution reports.

## Desired Submission Format

Scarlet specifies desired submission formats for certain technical documentation to facilitate easier assessment. It is recommended that usability engineering evaluation documentation is provided as a standard document format, such as .DOCX or .PDF.

## Deep Dive

### Expected Content: Formative Evaluation Reports

#### Responsible Personnel

Include:

- The identification of the personnel responsible for the usability engineering formative evaluation report and evidence of their competency for this activity.

#### Activity Reports

For each planned usability engineering formative evaluation activity, include:

- Indicate the personnel who conducted the activity and provide evidence of their competency for this activity.
- Indicate if the activity was conducted according to the plans and describe any deviations.
- Describe the results and conclusions of the activity.
- Describe any new risk elements discovered during the activity. This may include new hazard-related use scenarios, use errors, use difficulties, hazards or hazardous situations.

#### Concluding Remarks

Provide concluding remarks that:

- Indicate the analysis of the user interface design and improvements required to remediate any new risk elements that were discovered during any of the usability engineering formative evaluation activities. If it is deemed that improvements were not required or feasible, justify this conclusion.

### Expected Content: Summative Evaluation Reports

#### Responsible Personnel

Include:

- The identification of the personnel responsible for the usability engineering summative evaluation report and evidence of their competency for this activity.

#### Activity Reports

For each planned usability engineering summative evaluation activity, include:

- Indicate the personnel who conducted the activity and provide evidence of their competency for this activity.
- Indicate if the activity was conducted according to the plans and describe any deviations.
- Describe the results and conclusions of the activity.
- Describe any new risk elements discovered during the activity. This may include new hazard-related use scenarios, use errors, use difficulties, hazards or hazardous situations.

#### Concluding Remarks

Provide concluding remarks that:

- Indicate the analysis of the user interface design and improvements required to remediate any new risk elements that were discovered during any of the usability engineering summative evaluation activities. If it is deemed that improvements were not required or feasible, justify this conclusion.
- Provide evidence that the residual risk of the medical device remains acceptable following the conclusion of the usability engineering activities.
- Indicate whether the use errors and hazard-related use scenarios have been adequately evaluated, and the medical device's user interface has been deemed safe to use.

#### Traceability

Include:

- Traceability of usability-related use requirements (e.g. interface requirements and user training requirements) to usability summative evaluation activities.
- Traceability of usability-related risk elements (e.g. use errors, hazard-related use scenarios and _"information for safety"_ risk control measures) to usability summative evaluation activities.
- Traceability of usability summative evaluation activities to usability summative evaluation activity reports.
