# GSPR Checklist

Include a GSPR checklist that demonstrates maturity and facilitates regulatory review.

GSPR stands for **General Safety and Performance Requirements**. These requirements outline the safety, performance, risk management, usability, and cybersecurity expectations for medical devices.

A **GSPR checklist** is a structured, item-by-item demonstration of how your product meets each of the applicable requirements. It links each clause of the regulation to evidence within your technical documentation.

## Minimum Requirements

A complete GSPR checklist typically includes the following information:

- A **reference** to each applicable GSPR clause, along with the **full text** if desired.
- A declaration of **applicability** (e.g., "Yes", "No", or "Not Applicable").
- A **justification** for non-applicable requirements (e.g., requirements related to sterilisation, implantables, or biocompatibility).
- The **method(s) used to demonstrate conformity**.
- The **harmonised standards, Common Specifications (CS) or other solutions** applied.
- A **reference to the evidence** that demonstrates conformity (e.g., risk files, test reports, clinical data, design documentation).

## Desired Format

A **tabular format** -- usually in a spreadsheet -- is ideal. It allows for:

- Easy filtering and sorting.
- Clear visibility for reviewers.
- Direct linking to sections or documents in your technical file or eQMS.

Tables can be built in Excel or Google Sheets or generated directly from your electronic QMS (eQMS) or document management system.
