# ISO 13485 Certification

ISO 13485:2016 specifies requirements for a medical device quality management system (QMS). Certification verifies that your QMS is implemented and maintained.

This article outlines the general expectations for quality management system (QMS) documentation that manufacturers should prepare when applying for initial ISO 13485 certification through Scarlet. These materials demonstrate compliance with ISO 13485:2016 requirements related to quality assurance, safety, and regulatory readiness.

While ISO 13485 defines the required outcomes, it is the manufacturer's responsibility to establish, document, implement, and maintain appropriate processes and procedures that reflect their specific operations.

## QMS Requirements

> **Important Disclaimer:** This is not an exhaustive list. Requirements may vary depending on the organisation's size, the nature of the medical device, and its risk classification.

The following table outlines the key processes and documented procedures typically expected under ISO 13485:2016 for an initial QMS assessment:

| Process / Procedure | Description |
|---|---|
| Quality manual | High-level document describing the QMS structure, scope, exclusions, and interactions between processes. |
| Medical device file | A compilation of documentation demonstrating conformity to product-specific requirements. |
| Document and record control | Procedures to manage the creation, review, approval, distribution, and retention of QMS documents and records. |
| Quality policy | A documented statement of the organisation's commitment to quality, aligned with regulatory and business objectives. |
| Quality objectives | Measurable goals derived from the quality policy, maintained and reviewed to drive improvement. |
| Management review process | A documented procedure for management reviews, including input/output requirements and meeting records. |
| Responsibility and authority | Documentation assigning responsibilities and authorities to ensure effective QMS operation. |
| Internal audits | Internal audit planning, execution, and follow-up to ensure QMS compliance and effectiveness. |
| Communication | Procedures to manage internal communication and communication with customers and regulators, ensuring effective information flow. |
| Customer-related processes | Procedures for determining and reviewing customer requirements, including order handling, feedback, and satisfaction. |
| Resource management | Documentation related to human resources, infrastructure, and work environment needed for product conformity. |
| Risk management process | Process in accordance with ISO 14971 covering hazard identification, risk estimation, evaluation, control, and monitoring. |
| Design and development control | Design procedures including planning, inputs, outputs, reviews, verification, validation, and design changes (where applicable). |
| Supplier & purchasing controls | Criteria for supplier evaluation, selection, monitoring, and re-evaluation, including records of purchasing information. |
| Product development and release controls | Controlled conditions for software development and release, including validation of software (e.g. configuration, verification, deployment), control of special processes (such as automated build or testing pipelines), and documented oversight of outsourced activities. |
| Installation and servicing | Procedures for installation and servicing of devices, where applicable, including verification and record-keeping requirements. |
| Product identification and traceability | Procedures for identifying products throughout production and ensuring traceability. |
| Monitoring and measurement | Procedures for evaluating whether QMS processes and final product outputs meet defined requirements, using a combination of metrics, audits, reviews, and test results, inspection procedures, and controls for nonconforming product. |
| Corrective and preventive action (CAPA) | Procedures for identifying and eliminating root causes of actual and potential nonconformities. |
| Customer feedback and complaints | Feedback and complaint-handling procedures, including investigation, trending, and regulatory reporting. |
| Control of nonconforming product | Process for identifying and controlling nonconforming product to prevent unintended use or delivery. |
| Analysis of data | Procedures for collecting and analysing data from QMS processes, audits, complaints, and other inputs to support management review and continuous improvement. |

## Typical ISO 13485 Certification Flow

### Pre-submission

1. Build and implement your QMS
2. Define your QMS scope and process map
3. Run internal audits and management review
4. Fix gaps with CAPA where needed

### Submission and Assessment

1. Submit your QMS documentation and application details
2. Stage 1 audit (readiness)
3. Stage 2 audit (full assessment)
4. Resolve nonconformities (if raised)
5. Final review and decision making (FRDM)

### Post-certification

1. Surveillance audits (every 12 months)
2. Unannounced audits (at least once every 5 years)
3. Ongoing maintenance (internal audit, CAPA, management review)
4. Recertification audit (before expiry)

For more information on audits, please see the Audits page.

## Additional Guidance for Applicants

Manufacturers are encouraged to:

- Align QMS procedures with ISO 13485 clauses to avoid duplication and ensure coherence.
- Conduct a gap assessment prior to submission to identify missing procedures, misalignments, or outdated practices.
- Provide traceability between procedures, records, and the device's lifecycle -- this helps demonstrate control over design, production, post-market phases, and feedback.
- Ensure objective evidence is available for all applicable requirements. Auditors must be able to clearly see what was done, by whom, when, and in what sequence. This includes completed forms, logs, training records, meeting minutes, validation reports, audit trails, etc. It is essential to understand that it is not enough to describe a process -- it must be implemented, documented, and demonstrable.
