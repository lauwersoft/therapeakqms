# Post-Market Clinical Follow-Up

> Create a PMCF plan that confirms the ongoing clinical performance and safety of your device.

The Post-Market Clinical Follow-Up (PMCF) Plan outlines the methods and activities a manufacturer undertakes to gather clinical data on a medical device that is already CE or UKCA marked.

This plan is a legal requirement under the EU/UK MDR and a vital component of the post-market surveillance (PMS) system.

## Minimum Requirements

Manufacturers are required to provide enough information to allow notified bodies to evaluate the scientific and methodological rigour of the proposed activities.

- **Each activity**: Specify the type of activity (e.g., literature review, registry analysis, survey, clinical investigation), the objective it supports, and why it is appropriate.
- **Literature reviews**: Describe the databases to be searched, inclusion and exclusion criteria, search terms, timeframes, and the method for assessing the relevance and quality of evidence.
- **Clinical investigations or registries**: Define the study design, endpoints, sample size justification, recruitment strategy, data collection methods, and statistical analysis plan.
- **Surveys or feedback collection**: Outline the target population, sampling method, content and structure of the questionnaire, frequency, and the approach to analysing responses.
- **Clear linkage to clinical evaluation gaps**: Explain how each activity addresses specific uncertainties or data needs identified in the Clinical Evaluation Plan.
- **Risks and benefits**: Describe how the PMCF activities relate to residual risks and ongoing benefit-risk assessment, including how they help identify new risks.

## Desired Format

Post market surveillance documentation, such as PMCF plans/reports and PMS plans/reports are typically narrative in nature. Provide them as text files.

We want to see details of the associated clinical evaluation plan to demonstrate alignment between pre- and post-market clinical strategies.

## Deep Dive

### General Information

There are no surprises here. These same details will feature throughout the technical file.

- **Date and version** - specify the date on which the PMCF Plan was created and its version.
- **Revision history** - provide details on the revision history of the plan including the authors who contributed to revisions.
- **Medical device** - specify the medical device to which the plan pertains.

### Activities

Activities can include (non-exhaustive list):

- Literature searches.
- Post-market studies.
- Registry data.
- Survey data from health care professionals.
- Survey data from patients and/or users.

Planned activities should include details on design, specific timelines, objectives, hypotheses, sample size, analysis plans, endpoints, and inclusion/exclusion criteria. Rationale and limitations for each chosen activity should also be included.

#### Confirming Safety and Performance

Define the activities that will be used to confirm that the device continues to perform as intended and remains clinically safe in the post-market setting.

#### Identifying New Clinical Risks

Detail the methods you will use to detect new clinical risks that may emerge during broader clinical use.

#### Analysing Existing Clinical Risks

Include activities that will re-evaluate previously identified risks based on real-world evidence to detect changes in their frequency or severity.

#### Identifying Unknown Side Effects and Contraindications

Provide details on how previously unknown side-effects will be identified, and how known side-effects and contraindications will be monitored.

#### Evaluate Continued Acceptability of Benefit-Risk Ratio

Describe how you will assess whether the benefit-risk balance remains favourable based on the ongoing clinical data collected. This should include relevant information from the Clinical Evaluation Report (CER) and Risk Management File.

#### Identifying Misuse or Off-Label Use

Include mechanisms to detect and analyse situations where the device is used improperly or outside of its intended scope.

#### Evaluating Clinical Data Relating to Similar Devices

Include evaluation of similar devices where equivalence cannot be fully claimed, but insights may be relevant for context or safety comparisons.

#### Evaluating Clinical Data Relating to Alternative Treatment Options

Provide details of any documents that provide comparisons of your device's performance and safety against other available treatment options to contextualise its continued clinical relevance and value.
