## 1. Purpose

This Post-Market Clinical Follow-Up (PMCF) Plan defines the methods, activities, and timelines by which SkintelligentAI will proactively collect and evaluate clinical data on EpiFlare following its placement on the market. The plan is intended to confirm the ongoing safety and clinical performance of the device, to address residual uncertainties identified during the pre-market clinical evaluation, and to support the continued acceptability of the benefit-risk profile throughout the device lifecycle.

This document has been prepared in accordance with:

- Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Article 61(11) and Annex XIV Part B.
- MDCG 2020-7: Post-market clinical follow-up (PMCF) Plan Template.
- MEDDEV 2.12/2 Rev. 2: Post-market clinical follow-up studies.

## 5. Clinical Evaluation Context

### 5.1 Summary of Pre-Market Clinical Evidence

The pre-market clinical evaluation, documented in the Clinical Evaluation Report (SAI-EF-CER-001), was conducted in accordance with MEDDEV 2.7/1 Rev. 4 and MDR Annex XIV Part A. Clinical evidence was generated from the following sources:

- **Systematic literature review:** Supporting evidence on the clinical state of the art, disease monitoring approaches, and similar devices.
- **Retrospective image database study (N = 500):** Under controlled acquisition conditions, EpiFlare demonstrated strong analytical performance with an RMSE of 1.8 IDM units, 78.4% of measurements within +/-2 IDM units of the reference standard, sensitivity of 90.0% and specificity of 97.0% for high-risk detection (IDM > 99).
- **Prospective clinical performance study (N = 220):** Under conditions intended to approximate real-world use (clinic waiting room), performance remained acceptable but with increased variability: RMSE of 2.6 IDM units, 62.3% of measurements within +/-2 IDM units, sensitivity of 85.7% and specificity of 93.0% for high-risk detection (IDM > 99).

The clinical evaluation concluded that EpiFlare demonstrates an acceptable safety profile, meets predefined performance criteria, and provides a favourable benefit-risk balance when used as intended.

### 5.2 Evidence Gaps and Residual Uncertainties

The following evidence gaps and residual uncertainties were identified in the CER and are to be addressed through the PMCF activities defined in this plan:

| ID | Evidence gap / residual uncertainty | Source |
|---|---|---|
| PMCF-G01 | Performance under fully real-world home-use conditions has not been validated; the prospective study was conducted in a simulated real-world setting. | CER Section 12 (Evaluation limitations) |
| PMCF-G02 | Pre-market datasets include a higher proportion of patients with moderate to higher disease activity, introducing potential spectrum bias. Performance across the full disease severity spectrum, including very mild disease, is less well characterised. | CER Section 8.4 (Clinical relevance and imperfections) |
| PMCF-G03 | Long-term clinical performance stability and safety data are not yet available; the pre-market evaluation primarily reflects short- to medium-term use. | CER Section 12 (Evaluation limitations) |
| PMCF-G04 | Clinical benefit in patients with very mild or borderline disease activity is less well characterised. | CER Section 9.4 (Population-level considerations) |
| PMCF-G05 | Potential for previously unknown side effects, new risks, or off-label use to emerge during broader real-world clinical use. | CER Section 10.3 (Emerging and new risks) |

## 7. PMCF Activities

### 7.1 Activity 1: Post-Market Clinical Performance Study

**Type of activity:** Prospective, multi-centre, observational real-world performance study.

**Objective(s) supported:** PMCF-O01, PMCF-O02, PMCF-O03, PMCF-O04, PMCF-O05, PMCF-O06, PMCF-O07.

**Rationale:** The pre-market prospective clinical performance study was conducted in a simulated real-world setting (clinic waiting room) rather than in fully unsupervised home-use conditions. This study is designed to confirm that EpiFlare maintains acceptable performance when used as intended in routine clinical practice, including home-based image capture by patients without direct supervision. This activity directly addresses the most significant residual uncertainties identified in the CER.

**Study design:**

Prospective, non-interventional, observational study in patients using EpiFlare as part of routine clinical care. Participants will use EpiFlare for home-based image capture and IDM monitoring as part of their standard CDD management, supervised by their treating healthcare professional. IDM values generated by EpiFlare will be compared with periodic DIAU reference standard assessments performed during scheduled clinic visits.

**Study population:**

- Adult patients (>=18 years) with a confirmed diagnosis of CDD.
- Recruitment will ensure representation across the full disease severity spectrum, including patients with mild, moderate, and severe disease activity.
- Patients across multiple clinical sites and geographic locations within the EU will be enrolled to ensure population diversity.

**Inclusion criteria:**

- Adults aged >=18 years with a confirmed CDD diagnosis.
- Currently managed with EpiFlare as part of routine clinical care.
- Willing and able to perform home-based image capture using a compatible smartphone.
- Able to provide written informed consent.

**Exclusion criteria:**

- Age under 18 years.
- Absence of a confirmed CDD diagnosis.
- Concurrent skin conditions that substantially confound CDD disease activity assessment.
- Inability to use EpiFlare as intended.

**Sample size justification:** A minimum of 300 participants will be enrolled, with a target of at least 500 paired observations over the 12-month follow-up period. This sample size is selected to provide sufficient statistical power to detect clinically meaningful differences in performance compared to the pre-market prospective study and to enable subgroup analyses by disease severity. Assuming an RMSE of approximately 3.0 IDM units and a standard deviation of differences of approximately 3.5 IDM units, 500 paired observations will yield a 95% confidence interval width of approximately +/-0.3 IDM units for the RMSE estimate. This level of precision is considered adequate to detect performance degradation relative to pre-market benchmarks and to characterise performance across disease severity subgroups.

**Endpoints:**

- **Primary:** RMSE of EpiFlare IDM values compared with DIAU reference standard, under real-world home-use conditions.
- **Secondary:**
  - Percentage of measurements within +/-2 IDM units of the reference standard.
  - Sensitivity and specificity for high-risk detection (IDM > 99).
  - Subgroup analysis by disease severity (mild, moderate, severe).
  - Incidence of device-related adverse events.
  - Identification of new risks, off-label use, or misuse patterns.

**Acceptance criteria:**

- RMSE <= 3.0 IDM units (consistent with pre-market expectations for real-world use).
- Sensitivity >= 80% and specificity >= 85% for high-risk detection (IDM > 99).
- No new unacceptable safety signals.

**Data collection methods:**

- EpiFlare IDM outputs will be collected automatically via the device backend.
- DIAU reference measurements will be obtained during scheduled clinic visits (approximately every 3 months).
- Adverse event data will be collected via the clinical interface and periodic site reporting.
- Patterns of use, including frequency and timing of image capture, will be monitored.

**Statistical analysis plan:**

- RMSE, mean bias, and 95% limits of agreement (Bland-Altman analysis) for continuous IDM agreement.
- Sensitivity, specificity, PPV, and NPV for threshold-based classification performance.
- Pre-specified subgroup analyses by disease severity, skin type, age group, and clinical site.
- Descriptive analysis of adverse events, device deficiencies, and use patterns.

**Timeline:** Study initiation within 6 months of device market placement; 12-month follow-up period per participant; interim analysis at 6 months.

**Limitations:** As a non-interventional observational study, this activity is subject to the inherent limitations of uncontrolled real-world data, including variability in image quality and patient compliance. These limitations are acknowledged and will be addressed through data quality monitoring and sensitivity analyses.

**Ethics and regulatory:** The study will be conducted in accordance with ISO 14155:2020, the Declaration of Helsinki, and applicable national regulations. Ethics committee approval will be obtained prior to participant enrolment.

### 7.2 Activity 2: Literature Review

**Type of activity:** Ongoing systematic literature review.

**Objective(s) supported:** PMCF-O04, PMCF-O05, PMCF-O06, PMCF-O08, PMCF-O09.

**Rationale:** Continuous monitoring of the published scientific literature is essential to identify new evidence relevant to the safety, performance, and clinical benefit of EpiFlare and to ensure the clinical evaluation remains current with the evolving state of the art.

**Databases to be searched:** PubMed/MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane Library, clinical trial registries (ClinicalTrials.gov), regulatory databases (EUDAMED, MHRA), professional society websites (EADV, BAD).

**Inclusion and exclusion criteria:** Consistent with the criteria defined in the pre-market Literature Search Protocol (SAI-EF-LSP-001), updated as necessary to reflect the evolving clinical and regulatory landscape.

**Search terms:** Search terms will be based on those defined in the Literature Search Protocol (SAI-EF-LSP-001) and will include terms relating to CDD, EpiFlare, inflammatory skin disease monitoring, image-based disease assessment, software medical devices for dermatology, IDM, and disease monitoring tools. Search strategies will be updated to capture newly emerging terminology or technologies.

**Timeframes:** Searches will be conducted annually, with the first post-market search conducted within 12 months of market placement.

**Quality assessment:** Identified literature will be appraised using the methodology defined in the pre-market CEP, including methodological quality assessment using CASP checklists and relevance appraisal.

**Scope of review:** The literature review will specifically address:

- Changes in the clinical state of the art for CDD monitoring and management.
- New evidence on the safety and clinical performance of comparable devices.
- Emerging risks, adverse events, or contraindications associated with image-based monitoring devices.
- New information on alternative treatment options and their comparative effectiveness.
- Evidence relating to similar devices, including recalls, safety notices, and vigilance data.

**Timeline:** Annual review cycle, aligned with the clinical evaluation update cycle and PSUR reporting.

### 7.3 Activity 3: Healthcare Professional Survey

**Type of activity:** Structured survey of healthcare professionals.

**Objective(s) supported:** PMCF-O01, PMCF-O04, PMCF-O05, PMCF-O06, PMCF-O07.

**Rationale:** Direct feedback from healthcare professionals who use EpiFlare in clinical practice provides qualitative and semi-quantitative insights into device performance, usability, clinical utility, and any emerging concerns that may not be captured through quantitative performance data alone.

**Target population:** Dermatologists and primary care physicians actively using EpiFlare to manage patients with CDD.

**Sampling method:** All registered healthcare professional users of EpiFlare will be invited to participate. A minimum response rate of 30% will be targeted.

**Content and structure:** The survey will include questions addressing:

- Clinical utility and integration into routine practice.
- Perceived accuracy and reliability of IDM outputs and alerts.
- Instances of device outputs that were inconsistent with clinical assessment.
- Observed or suspected off-label use or misuse by patients.
- Any adverse events or near-miss events not previously reported.
- Suggestions for improvement or additional clinical needs.

The survey will employ a combination of Likert-scale, multiple-choice, and free-text response formats.

**Frequency:** Annually, commencing 12 months after market placement.

**Approach to analysis:** Quantitative responses will be summarised descriptively (frequencies, proportions). Free-text responses will be reviewed thematically and coded for emerging safety signals, usability issues, or patterns of off-label use. Results will be cross-referenced with PMS complaint data and adverse event records.

### 7.4 Activity 4: Patient Experience Survey

**Type of activity:** Structured survey of patients.

**Objective(s) supported:** PMCF-O01, PMCF-O04, PMCF-O05, PMCF-O07.

**Rationale:** Patient feedback is essential to understanding the real-world user experience, identifying barriers to correct device use, detecting potential misuse, and identifying adverse experiences that may not be reported through formal complaint channels.

**Target population:** Adult patients (>=18 years) with a confirmed CDD diagnosis who are actively using EpiFlare for home-based image capture and disease monitoring.

**Sampling method:** All registered patient users of EpiFlare will be invited to participate via the mobile application. A minimum response rate of 20% will be targeted.

**Content and structure:** The survey will include questions addressing:

- Ease of use for image capture and upload.
- Understanding of IDM results and alert notifications.
- Any unexpected experiences, symptoms, or concerns related to device use.
- Adherence to image acquisition instructions and frequency of use.
- Understanding of the device's role as a decision-support tool requiring healthcare professional interpretation.
- Any use of the device outside of its intended purpose.

**Frequency:** Annually, commencing 12 months after market placement.

**Approach to analysis:** Quantitative responses will be summarised descriptively. Free-text responses will be reviewed thematically and coded for emerging safety signals, usability issues, or off-label use. Results will be cross-referenced with HCP survey findings and PMS data.
