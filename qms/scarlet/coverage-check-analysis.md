# Coverage Check Analysis

In-app tooling that provides directional feedback by highlighting where expected concepts appear in your documentation.

The coverage check tool is a private sandbox for draft technical documentation. It helps you:

- Understand how well your technical documentation aligns with Scarlet's guidance and best practices
- Find where expected concepts may appear in your documents
- Track progress across different work streams (Clinical, Device Information, Post-Market Surveillance, Risk/Usability, Software)
- Access direct links to relevant Knowledge Base articles for each item

The coverage check tool gives directional feedback only. It does not judge quality, completeness, or regulatory sufficiency.

## What You'll See in the Report

The coverage report will provide:

- A top-level coverage score out of 100
- Five category scores, each also out of 100:
  - Clinical
  - Device Information
  - Post-Market Surveillance
  - Risk/Usability
  - Software
- A radar chart showing how your score is distributed across categories
- Access direct links to Knowledge Base articles for each item

Each score reflects the presence of expected items.

## How to Use It

1. Navigate to the coverage checker in your app
2. Upload your documents and run

### Behind the Scenes

- Files are currently filtered to include only relevant PDFs (e.g., excluding templates or unsupported formats), though support for additional formats is planned in future versions
- The tool uses Scarlet's Knowledge Base as a reference
- A semantic search is run to identify where expected items appear in your documents
- The Scarlet product and customer experience teams may conduct a brief check to ensure the results are usable and useful before sharing

## What This Tool Does

- Identifies where expected concepts, based on Scarlet's Knowledge Base, appear in your uploaded files
- Surfaces results at three levels: category, subcategory, and checklist item
- Provides a directional view of alignment with Scarlet's published materials
- Offers links to relevant Knowledge Base articles for further reference

## What This Tool Does NOT Do

- It does not assess the quality, completeness, or traceability of your content
- It does not evaluate regulatory sufficiency
- It does not provide content review, gap analysis, specific regulatory advice or feedback, or consultation

This is a pilot tool and is still evolving. It may yield false positives or false negatives. A 100% identification score does not imply certification or eliminate the possibility of queries or nonconformities during the assessment.

It's possible that the Coverage Report will provide a top level score of 100, but the results of our formal application review (or assessment) show an incomplete submission. As the manufacturer, the responsibility to submit a complete application rests entirely with you.
