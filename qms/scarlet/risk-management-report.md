# Risk Management Report

> Create a risk management report that demonstrates a rigorous approach to benefit-risk evaluation.

A risk management report concludes the risk management activities required to support the technical submission of the medical device. The document confirms that all risk management activities have been conducted, all risk mitigations have been implemented and verified and that the overall residual risk evaluation has determined the device to be safe.

## Minimum Requirements

Include the following information in your risk management report:

- **Personnel responsible** - Specify those involved in the generation of the risk management report and detail their competency: education, professional training, skill, and work experience related to risk management activities.
- **Completion of activities** - Confirm that all risk management activities have been completed, including the identification, evaluation and control of all relevant risks.
- **Benefit-risk analysis** - If required, include a statement justifying that any unacceptable residual risk is outweighed by the clinical benefits.
- **Overall residual risk evaluation** - Documenting the outcomes of one or more overall residual risk evaluation activities that collectively evaluate the residual risk of all risk sequences. Include:
  - An indication of the personnel responsible for the overall residual risk evaluation.
  - Confirmation of completeness, implementation and effectiveness of all risk controls.
  - Confirmation of the residual risk of all risk sequences after adding risk control.
  - References to any benefit-risk analysis activities conducted for unmitigable risks.
- **Risk management process review** - Specify who will perform a review of risk management processes and justify that they have the appropriate authority to do so, before confirming the criteria to be reviewed. These may include:
  - The risk acceptability criteria policy is still suitable.
  - The risk management process has adequate, competent resources.
  - The risk management process has been appropriately implemented.
  - The post-market surveillance activities adequately support risk management activities in the production phase and collect safety-related data.
- **Conclusion** - Answer, with justification, the following questions:
  - Has the risk management plan been appropriately implemented?
  - Is the overall residual risk acceptable?
  - Are there appropriate methods in place to collect and review data in the post-market phase?

## Common Pitfalls

Here are three common issues we encounter with the _Risk management report_.

### Inadequate Evaluation

Many manufacturers treat the _Risk management report_ as simply a compilation of individual risk assessments without providing the critical overall evaluation required by ISO 14971. There is no statement on the final overall residual risk or benefit-risk assessment and, in particular, how and with which method this is carried out.

### Absence of Risk Management Process Review

Often the process review is either skipped entirely or treated as a cursory checkbox exercise without genuine critical evaluation of whether risk management activities were adequate.

### Missing Details of Post-Market Review Processes

Not having a risk management plan in place for the post-market stage of their device will cause significant problems when complaints and nonconformances arise. Often risk management is treated as a pre-market activity only, failing to describe how risks will be monitored and re-evaluated after market release.

## Desired Format

Narrative documents, such as the _Risk management plan_ and the _Risk management report_, should be submitted as text documents.

- Use text files (such as .DOCX, .PDF) for your main reports: _Risk management plan_ and _Risk management report_.
- Use spreadsheets (such as .XLSX) for detailed data like hazards, risk estimations, and controls. This keeps everything clear and easy to trace.
