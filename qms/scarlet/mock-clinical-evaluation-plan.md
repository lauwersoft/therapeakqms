## 1. Purpose

This Clinical Evaluation Plan (CEP) describes the methodology and processes by which clinical evidence will be identified, appraised, and analysed to demonstrate the clinical safety, performance, and benefit-risk profile of EpiFlare. EpiFlare is a Class IIa medical device intended to support qualified healthcare professionals in monitoring disease activity and assessing flare severity in adult patients with confirmed Cox-Dewar Dermatitis (CDD).

This document is prepared in accordance with:

- Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Article 61 and Annex XIV Part A
- MEDDEV 2.7/1 Rev. 4

## 3. Device Description

- Device name: EpiFlare
- Version: 2.0
- Basic UDI-DI: (01)00000000000000
- Classification: Class IIa
- Intended purpose: EpiFlare is intended to be used by qualified healthcare professionals, including dermatologists and primary care physicians, to support the monitoring of disease activity in adult patients (>=18 years) with a confirmed diagnosis of Cox-Dewar Dermatitis (CDD). ## 6. Risk Management

A risk analysis, conducted in compliance with EN ISO 14971:2019, is documented in:

- Risk Management Plan.

- Risk Management Report.

Risk management documentation is an input for the clinical evaluation and clinical evaluation results serve as an input for risk assessment, i.e., hazards, risks, and side-effects identified in the clinical evaluation undergo evaluation in the risk management file. A full benefit-risk determination is documented in the Clinical Evaluation Report.

## 7. Clinical State of the Art

### 7.1 Objective

To identify and characterise the current clinical state of the art relevant to monitoring of Cox-Dewar Dermatitis, in order to contextualise the device's intended purpose, performance expectations, and benefit-risk profile.

### 7.2 Methodology

The clinical state of the art will be established through a structured literature review conducted in accordance with a predefined Literature Search Protocol. The assessment will include:

- Disease characteristics, natural history, and epidemiology of CDD
- Current monitoring practices and standards of care
- Accepted clinical parameters used to assess disease activity and progression
- Known limitations and variability of existing monitoring approaches
- Identified unmet clinical needs

The results of the literature search and appraisal will be documented in a Literature Search Report and synthesised in the CER.

## 8. Benchmarks and Acceptability Criteria

### 8.1 Purpose

To define safety and performance benchmarks against which the device's clinical data will be evaluated.

### 8.2 Methodology

Benchmarks will be derived from:

- Published clinical guidelines
- Peer-reviewed validation studies
- Widely accepted reference standards
- Clinical expert input
- Clinical investigation(s)

The following categories of benchmarks will be defined prior to data appraisal:

- Technical performance benchmarks (e.g. accuracy, agreement, reliability)
- Clinical performance benchmarks relevant to disease monitoring and flare identification
- Safety benchmarks, including comparison with standard monitoring approaches

These benchmarks will be used as predefined acceptability criteria during clinical data appraisal and benefit-risk assessment. Benchmark justification and sources will be documented in the CER.

## 9. Similar and Equivalent Devices

### 9.1 Objective

To identify and characterise devices or systems with similar intended purposes or clinical functions, in order to inform expectations regarding performance, safety, and clinical utility.

### 9.2 Methodology

- A systematic literature review will be conducted to identify similar devices used for monitoring inflammatory skin conditions.
- Equivalence will only be claimed where all applicable MDR equivalence criteria are met. For EpiFlare, no equivalence claim is planned.
- For similar devices, the following aspects will be extracted and compared where available:
  - Intended purpose and clinical context
  - Measurement principles
  - Reported performance characteristics
  - Safety considerations
- Findings will be used to contextualise clinical expectations and define evaluation criteria, not to substitute for device-specific clinical evidence.

## 10. Clinical Benefits

EpiFlare provides clinical benefit by supporting:

- Objective monitoring of disease activity in adult patients with Cox-Dewar Dermatitis (CDD) through quantitative IDM values derived from serial skin images.
- Identification of patients at increased risk of disease flare, using predefined IDM thresholds and trend-based changes to prompt timely clinical review.
- Longitudinal assessment of disease trends over time, enabling clinicians to detect changes in disease activity that may not be captured through episodic clinic-based assessment alone.

The clinical evaluation will demonstrate the relevance, magnitude, and clinical significance of these benefits using predefined outcome measures, performance benchmarks, and acceptability criteria derived from the clinical state of the art, scientific literature, and clinical expert input.

## 11. Outcome Measures and Evaluation Criteria

### 11.1 Outcome Measures

Outcome measures for the clinical evaluation will be defined a priori based on the identified state of the art, taking into account relevant scientific literature, applicable standards, guidance documents, and clinical practice for monitoring and management of CDD.

The selection of outcome measures will be informed by:

- Findings from systematic literature searches addressing clinical association, performance, and safety;
- Reported performance metrics and endpoints used for comparable technologies or approaches, where available;
- Clinically meaningful endpoints reflecting disease activity, flare identification, and longitudinal monitoring;
- Regulatory and methodological guidance on appropriate performance and safety evaluation of medical devices.

Planned outcome measures may therefore include, as appropriate:

- Performance metrics comparing device output to a defined clinical reference standard;
- Measures of agreement, correlation, and bias;
- Classification performance metrics at clinically relevant, pre-specified thresholds.

### 11.2 Methodology

- Outcome measures and associated acceptability criteria will be predefined prior to analysis and justified with reference to clinical relevance and state-of-the-art evidence.
- Statistical methods appropriate to the selected outcome measures and data type will be specified in advance.
- Where clinically relevant, subgroup analyses may be planned to assess the consistency of performance across relevant patient or use-condition subgroups.

## 12. Clinical Evaluation Activities

This section addresses the requirements specified in MEDDEV 2.7/1 Rev. 4, Section 7 (Clinical evaluation activities) and MDR Annex XIV Part A, which require manufacturers to plan activities for generating and collecting clinical data.

### 12.1 Clinical Association

**Purpose:** Demonstrate that EpiFlare output (IDM) corresponds to the clinical condition (CDD).

**Evidence sources:**

- Literature review: Clinical guidelines and validation studies establishing IDM as a clinical parameter

### 12.2 Clinical Performance

**Purpose:** Demonstrate that EpiFlare yields clinically relevant output and the clinical benefit is achieved.

**Evidence sources:**

- Image database study (retrospective)
- Prospective clinical performance study

### 12.3 Technical Performance

**Purpose:** Demonstrate that EpiFlare accurately, reliably, and precisely generates the intended output (IDM values) from the input data (skin images).

**Evidence sources:**

- Image database study (retrospective)
- Prospective clinical performance study

### 12.4 Retrospective Image Database Study

**Study design:** Retrospective analytical performance study using archived skin images with associated reference standard measurements.

**Objectives:** To establish baseline analytical accuracy of EpiFlare IDM values compared to the DIAU reference standard under controlled image acquisition conditions, prior to evaluation under conditions representative of intended use.

**Study population:** Archived skin images from adult patients (>=18 years) with confirmed Cox-Dewar Dermatitis (CDD), previously acquired using DIAU imaging systems at multiple clinical sites. Each image will have an associated reference IDM value measured at the time of image acquisition.

**Sample size justification:** The planned sample size is approximately 500 paired observations. This sample size is selected to ensure sufficient precision for estimating analytical accuracy and agreement between EpiFlare and the DIAU reference standard.

Based on an assumed root mean square error (RMSE) of approximately 2.0 IDM units and a standard deviation of differences of approximately 2.5 IDM units, a sample size of 500 paired observations is expected to provide a 95% confidence interval width of approximately +/-0.2-0.25 IDM units for the RMSE estimate. This level of precision is considered sufficient to support assessment against predefined analytical performance acceptability criteria.

In addition, assuming an expected prevalence of high-risk cases (IDM >99) of approximately 10-15%, a sample size of 500 is expected to yield a sufficient number of high-risk cases to enable reliable estimation of classification performance metrics (e.g. sensitivity and specificity), with acceptable confidence interval precision for the intended analytical performance evaluation.

**Methodology:** Archived images and corresponding DIAU-measured IDM values will be retrieved from a validated image database. Images will be selected to represent the full measurement range, a range of disease severities, and diverse skin tones. Each image will be processed using the EpiFlare algorithm to generate an IDM value, which will be compared to the corresponding DIAU reference value.

Planned analyses will include measures of analytical accuracy and agreement (e.g. RMSE, proportion within a predefined tolerance, Bland-Altman analysis) and classification performance for detection of high-risk cases at a predefined IDM threshold. Subgroup analyses will be planned to assess consistency of performance across relevant patient characteristics, including skin type.

**Limitations:** As images are acquired under controlled conditions using DIAU imaging systems, the results may not fully represent the variability in image quality and acquisition conditions encountered under intended use. This limitation will be addressed through a prospective clinical performance study conducted under conditions representative of intended use.

### 12.5 Prospective Clinical Performance Study

A prospective, multi-centre clinical performance study is planned to evaluate the clinical performance of EpiFlare under conditions representative of intended use.

The study will assess the ability of EpiFlare to generate clinically relevant IDM outputs in comparison with an appropriate clinical reference standard, using predefined outcome measures and acceptability criteria derived from the state of the art.

Detailed information on study design, study population, endpoints, sample size justification, statistical analysis, and operational procedures is specified in the Clinical Investigation Plan.
