# Instructions for Use

Craft instructions for use that equip patients and clinicians with the necessary information to take advantage of your technology.

Instructions for use (IFU) provide the necessary information for the user to understand the intended purpose, safe use, and precautions for a medical device or accessory.

The IFU covers key details related to the device itself, the manufacturer, clinical performance, safety information, and the technical information required to install, configure, operate, and maintain the device such that it remains safe and secure.

## Minimum Requirements

The IFU must include the following core information:

- **Device and manufacturer details** - Device identification, manufacturer information, authorised representative or responsible person details, and contact information.
- **Clinical performance** - Clinical benefits, performance characteristics, and for EU class III devices, the Summary of Safety and Clinical Performance (SSCP).
- **Safety information** - Residual risks, contraindications, warnings, disclaimers, precautions, adverse device effects, device deficiencies, and known limitations.
- **Technical information** - Installation, configuration, operation, and maintenance instructions, including hardware and software requirements, security settings, network specifications, and decommissioning procedures.
- **Training and qualifications** - User training requirements, qualification requirements, and when to consult healthcare professionals.
- **Operational procedures** - Preparatory steps, facility requirements, accessory and combination use, handling and storage, incident reporting procedures, and system messages.

A full list of expected content is provided in the Deep dive section.

## Common Pitfalls

Any mistakes in this document are easily fixed but avoiding these pitfalls will reduce the number of Request for information queries during the assessment process, making everyone happy.

> **Warning:**
>
> **Unclear organisational structure** - Sometimes the holding company is listed on the IFU when a subsidiary is the actual legal manufacturer or the legal manufacturer is omitted entirely in favour of a trading name.
>
> **Missing authorised representative details** - Under EU MDR, details of an authorised representative must be provided if the manufacturer is based outside of the EU.
>
> **Absence of performance metrics** - After clinical benefit claims, we expect to see algorithm performance metrics (e.g. sensitivity, specificity) and characteristics of the validation dataset.
>
> **Inadequate warnings** - Insufficient warnings about potential misinterpretation of outputs or missing warnings about use outside validated conditions could land you in bother.
>
> **Technical requirements** - Operating system versions explicitly supported, network/connectivity requirements and browser compatibility (for web-based SaMD) are key details that are sometimes omitted.
>
> **Training requirements** - Not specifying who needs training and to what level and omitting information about available training resources are common pitfalls.

## Desired Format

- Upload IFUs as stand-alone documents, identical in content and layout to the version delivered to the end user of the device.
- Use a commonly used electronic format (e.g. PDF) that preserves the visual layout.
- For devices with an electronic IFU (eIFU), include the URL or access point where the eIFU will be made available to users.
- Upload all language versions intended for delivery to the end user.

## Deep Dive

### Expected Content: General

- **Date of issue** - Enter the date the instructions for use were issued.
- **Device** - Define which medical device or accessory these instructions cover, including version number.
- **CE marking** - Provide the CE marking shown in the IFU if applicable.
- **Website** - Provide the link to the webpage where the eIFU and other relevant information are available.

### Expected Content: Manufacturer Details

Include the following details for the manufacturer of the device:

- Registered trade name
- Address (registered place of business)
- Details of any additional sites
- Company structure (describe the company structure, including any holding companies or subsidiaries of the manufacturer)
- Contact information of the main point of contact:
  - Name
  - Email address
  - Phone number
- Manufacturer website
- Registered trade name or mark - if applicable, input web link to the publicly available record of all associated registered trade marks(s) or registered trade name(s)
- Reference number - if applicable, provide the reference number for the manufacturer (e.g. Single Registration Number, SRN). Also include the name of the database associated with the number (e.g. EUDAMED)

**Authorised representative** - Under EU MDR, if the manufacturer is based outside the EU, provide details of the authorised representative. Full details of any authorised representative should be included in your Quality submission.

**Responsible person** - Under UK MDR, if the manufacturer is based outside the UK, provide details of the responsible person. Full details of any responsible person should be included in your Quality submission.

### Expected Content: Clinical Performance

- **Clinical investigation** - Indicate whether the device is used exclusively in a clinical investigation.
- **Clinical benefits** - Provide details of the clinical benefits of the device and/or accessory. This information is required for medical devices, optional for accessories.
- **Performance characteristics** - Provide details of the relevant clinical and technical performance data supporting the device's intended use.
- **Summary of safety and clinical performance (SSCP)** - For EU class III devices only, provide details of the most recent SSCP.

### Expected Content: Safety Information

- **Residual risks** - Refer to documented residual safety and security risks after mitigation measures.
- **Contraindications** - Refer to any contraindications associated with the device.
- **Warnings** - Refer to relevant warnings, including safety, security, and emergency considerations.
- **Disclaimers** - Provide details of any disclaimers relating to safety and security risks.
- **Precautions** - Refer to any precautions for use, including device setup, configuration, or environmental conditions.
- **Adverse device effects** - Describe or refer to known adverse effects related to the device's use.
- **Device deficiencies** - Describe or refer to known deficiencies that may impact performance or safety.
- **Limitations** - Describe known limitations not already addressed elsewhere.

### Expected Content: Training and Qualifications

- **Training requirements** - Describe any user training needed before using the device.
- **Qualification requirements** - Describe any qualifications or certifications required of the user and any IT skills needed for installation and configuration, when applicable.
- **Consulting healthcare professionals** - If the device is intended for lay use, describe when a user should consult a healthcare professional.

### Expected Content: Operational Procedures

- **Facility requirements and restrictions** - Detail any requirements for the location where the device will be used (e.g. IT network standards, physical environment).
- **Accessory and combination use** - List any other devices or equipment intended to be used with the device, including version numbers.
- **Preparatory steps** - Explain any steps required before using the device, such as calibration or software setup.
- **Maintenance** - Describe routine maintenance required to ensure the device performs safely and effectively.
- **Messages** - Provide descriptions of error or system messages that require user interpretation.
- **Serious incident reporting** - Include the fixed statements about reporting incidents to the manufacturer and competent authority. Then describe the specific procedure for doing so.
- **Special handling and storage** - Provide details of the label specifying handling and storage instructions.

### Expected Content: Technical Information

- **Installation statement** - State whether the user or a qualified person must install the device.
- **Hardware requirements and restrictions** - Describe the hardware specifications and configuration needed for secure, proper function.
- **Software requirements and restrictions** - Describe the software environment and configuration needed for secure, proper function.
- **Installation instructions** - Provide clear installation instructions, including any special requirements and security settings.
- **Installation verification** - Describe how to confirm successful and secure installation.
- **Critical dependencies** - List other applications the device relies on to function correctly.
- **Configuration requirements** - Describe how the device must be configured after installation.
- **System interface requirements and restrictions** - List required and optional interfaces and how the device interacts with other systems.
- **Start-up procedure** - Describe how to safely power on and initiate the device.
- **Shutdown procedure** - Describe how to safely shut down the device.
- **Operating instructions** - Provide the full set of instructions required to operate the device safely and effectively.
- **Security options** - List post-installation security settings, configuration options (e.g. password management, encryption, network ports) and initial default configuration values.
- **IT network specifications** - List technical specifications required for IT networks (e.g. Wi-Fi compatibility, firewall settings, bandwidth requirements).
- **Security alerts** - Provide action plans for a user to follow in case of an urgent security alert message.
- **Security upgrades** - Describe the process for applying security upgrades/patches.
- **Failure** - Explain what happens if the device detects a failure to maintain security and the impact on patients, users, or data.
- **Decommissioning** - Describe the process for safely uninstalling or retiring the device, including how to protect personal data.
