# Validation Report

Documentation that provides objective evidence of the execution of planned validation activities and indicates whether the SaMD's use requirements have been fulfilled.

Validation reports provide objective evidence of the execution of planned validation activities and indicate whether the SaMD has been validated for its intended medical purpose. Under IEC 82304-1, validation applies to the SaMD as a whole, including the software, its intended use, use environment, deployment configuration, and accompanying information (e.g. instructions for use, in-app guidance, training materials, and known limitations).

These reports document the actual execution of validation activities as defined in the validation plan, including the conditions and configurations under which activities were performed, any deviations from the plan, predefined acceptance criteria, results with supporting objective evidence, and any anomalies identified during execution.

Regulatory expectations under IEC 82304-1 and EU MDR technical documentation requirements require manufacturers to provide comprehensive validation reports demonstrating that the health software product has been successfully validated for its intended purpose. The validation report must include detailed execution reports for each validation activity, as well as overall validation conclusions that summarise results, provide evidence that residual risk remains acceptable, and confirm successful validation against the defined use requirements. This documentation supports the claim that the health software product is safe, secure, and effective for its intended use.

## Minimum Requirements

When submitting your technical documentation, the following are the minimum requirements for this topic:

- **Validation execution reports**: For each validation activity, the report must document the personnel who executed the activity, the conditions under which it was performed, whether it was performed according to the validation plan, any deviations, the result with supporting evidence, and any anomalies identified.
- **Validation conclusions**: The report must provide a summary of validation activity results, evidence that residual risk remains acceptable, and a conclusion on whether the medical device has been successfully validated for its intended purpose based on the defined use requirements.
- **Traceability**: Clear traceability must be provided from each use requirement to validation activities and evidence of the validation of use requirements.

## Common Pitfalls

The following are common queries that are raised by Scarlet during assessment:

> **Warning:**
>
> - **Lack of independent validation personnel**: Personnel executing the validation activities are not defined, or there is no evidence of their independence from the design and development of the medical device software.
> - **Inadequate validation outcomes**: Validation outcomes that lack sufficient evidence and rationale of the validation of use requirements being fulfilled. Without this, it is difficult to determine if the SaMD product is safe, secure, and effective for its intended purpose.

## Desired Submission Format

Scarlet specifies desired submission formats for certain technical documentation to facilitate easier assessment. It is recommended that validation reports are provided as a standard document format, such as .DOCX or .PDF. Supplementary documentation, such as validation protocols and execution reports, may be provided in tabular format.

## Deep Dive

### Expected Content: Validation Execution Reports

For each validation activity, include:

- Personnel who executed the activity, and confirmation of their independence from the design and development of the medical device software.
- Conditions of the validation activity performed.
- If the executions were performed according to the validation plans.
- Deviations from the plan, if applicable.
- Result of the execution, with supporting evidence to justify the result.
- Any anomalies identified during the execution.

### Expected Content: Validation Conclusions

Include:

- Summary of results of the validation activities.
- Evidence that the residual risk of the medical device remains acceptable.
- Whether the medical device has been successfully validated for its intended purpose based on the defined use requirements.
