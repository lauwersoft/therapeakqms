# Structured Dialogues

Structured dialogues are conversations between Scarlet and manufacturers applying for conformity assessment that relate to technical information and regulatory guidance. At Scarlet, structured dialogues are conducted online via video conferencing and provide a structured framework for discussing regulatory requirements before and during the certification process.

## How They Work

The structured dialogue process is designed to ensure efficient assessment:

1. **Preparation:** Before your structured dialogue, you will be provided with a Scarlet document template to populate. The template should contain a representative sample of your data for any given regulatory topic, not a complete list. This demonstrates your understanding of regulatory concepts and allows our team to review the information ahead of time.
2. **The dialogue:** Structured dialogues are conducted online via video conferencing. They are generally conducted on the basis of the template you've populated and supporting material Scarlet has made available covering key regulatory concepts. The guiding principle is that Scarlet will not provide specific advice -- conversations focus on "what needs to be fulfilled" rather than "how to fulfil".
3. **Outcome:** The information you provide for a structured dialogue is not part of your application. You may re-use and re-purpose some of the information, but the initial information provided will not be considered in your assessment. The goal is to help you prepare well-structured applications for efficient conformity assessments.

## The Benefits

The purpose of structured dialogues is to ensure efficient assessment:

- **Clear framework:** Offer a structured way for discussing regulatory requirements in an organised manner.
- **Early alignment:** Provide opportunities to discuss regulatory approval processes and expectations before an application is submitted.
- **Efficient submissions:** Facilitate the submission of well-prepared applications for efficient conformity assessments.
- **Regular touchpoints:** Introduce regular touchpoints in the application and assessment process to ensure ongoing alignment between your expectations and Scarlet's.
- **Right level of detail:** Help you focus on the right level of detail -- too little information leads to delays, and too much irrelevant data creates unnecessary complexity.

Structured dialogues are NOT a consultation service and are part of Scarlet's application and conformity assessment process.

## What Is Discussed

Manufacturers commonly use structured dialogues for topics such as:

- Device scope and classification
- Clinical foundations: Equivalence claims, clinical benefits, clinical performance and outcome measures, clinical state-of-the-art, clinical association, planned literature searches, similar devices, treatments and/or pathways, clinical data generation methods
- Post-market surveillance
- Software foundations: Use requirements, software requirements, software architecture & software safety classification, validation planning & activities
- Risk foundations: Device characteristics, risk traceability, including harms, hazards, hazardous situations and risk controls, usability engineering
- Quality Management System (QMS)
- Change requests
- Predetermined Change Control Plans (PCCPs)

## How Many Structured Dialogues Should You Expect?

Structured dialogues are typically held per device (i.e., each device has its own set of structured dialogues). Most manufacturers can expect 2-5 structured dialogues per device, depending on device complexity, readiness, and where they are in the process.

We generally avoid repeat structured dialogues on the same topic to mitigate consultancy risk and maintain clear boundaries.

## Making the Most of Your Structured Dialogues

To maximize the value of your structured dialogues with Scarlet:

- **Complete the template:** Provide a representative sample of your data, not a complete list. The detail provided may vary depending on your progress through the application process.
- **Submit ahead of time:** Send the completed template to Scarlet ahead of your structured dialogue to allow our team to review the information and make the call as productive as possible.
- **Understand the scope:** Remember that structured dialogues are discussions, not consultations. They promote efficiency and predictability by helping you understand and be prepared for the process of submission and assessment.

## Details on Preparing for Specific Structured Dialogues

### Preparing for Structured Dialogues: Clinical Foundations 1

The first clinical foundations structured dialogue will focus on the following topics:

- Equivalence claims, if relevant
- Clinical benefits, clinical performance and outcome measures
- Clinical state-of-the-art
- Clinical association
- Planned literature searches
- Similar devices, treatments and/or pathways

To facilitate discussion on each topic, you are requested to upload information/data related to this topic, along with any questions and/or clarifications that will support your understanding of these regulatory topics.

Please use the template provided in your Scarlet workspace to upload:

- A representative sample of the medical device's clinical benefit claims, associated outcome measures and performance claims.
- Details of the medical device's inputs and outputs, with an outline of the clinical evaluation activity to support each of these.
- A representative sample of research questions, with a purpose for each question and associated search terms.
- A representative sample of similar devices and/or alternative pathways that reflect the clinical state of the art, with performance/safety data where available.

### Preparing for Structured Dialogues: Clinical Foundations 2

The second Clinical Foundations Structured Dialogue will focus on the following topics:

- Clinical data generation methods
- Post-market surveillance

To facilitate discussion on each topic, you are requested to upload information/data related to this topic, along with any questions and/or clarifications that will support your understanding of these regulatory topics.

Please use the template provided in your Scarlet workspace to upload:

- An outline of one or more clinical investigations with study details, including inclusion/exclusion criteria, hypotheses and endpoints.
- An outline of post-market activities to ensure the safety and performance of the medical device during its lifetime.
- Details of processes to ensure the continued acceptability of the benefit-risk ratio.
- Details of methods to identify instances of device misuse, previously unknown side effects and serious incidents.
- Details of methods to monitor the state of the art, including similar and equivalent devices.

### Preparing for Structured Dialogues: Software Foundations

The software foundations structured dialogue will focus on the following topics:

- Use requirements
- Software requirements
- Software architecture & software safety classification
- Validation planning & activities

To facilitate discussion on each topic, you are requested to upload information/data related to this topic, along with any questions and/or clarifications that will support your understanding of these regulatory topics.

Please use the template provided in the request to upload:

- A representative sample of the medical device's use requirements and software requirements.
- The determination of the software safety classification.
- A representative sample of software modules and interfaces within the software architecture.
- A representative sample of the planned validation activities.

### Preparing for Structured Dialogues: Risk Foundations

The risk foundations structured dialogue will focus on the following topics:

- Device characteristics
- Risk traceability, including harms, hazards, hazardous situations and risk controls
- Usability engineering

To facilitate discussion on each topic, you are requested to upload information/data related to this topic, along with any questions and/or clarifications that will support your understanding of these regulatory topics.

Please use the template provided in the request to upload:

- A representative sample of the medical device's safety and security characteristics.
- A representative sample of the medical device's hazards, hazardous situations & harms.
- A representative sample of the medical device's hazardous sequences.
- A representative sample of the medical device's risk controls.
- A representative sample of the medical device's use scenarios and use errors.
- A representative sample of the medical device's usability engineering summative evaluation activities.
