### 1.4 Rationale for Qualification as a Medical Device

EpiFlare qualifies as a medical device in accordance with Article 2(1) of Regulation (EU) 2017/745 (EU MDR). EpiFlare is a software-based product intended to be used for a medical purpose, namely the monitoring and assessment of disease severity in patients with a known diagnosis of Cox-Dewar Dermatitis (CDD). Specifically, EpiFlare:

- analyses images of the skin acquired using a smartphone-based imaging setup; and
- provides a quantitative output (Inflammatory Decompensation Marker, IDM) to support identification of patients in whom treatment escalation (e.g. immunotherapy) may be indicated.

In accordance with Article 2(1) MDR, software is considered a medical device when it is intended for use in humans for purposes such as diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, prediction, prognosis, or alleviation of disease. EpiFlare meets these criteria as it is intended for monitoring and assessment of disease activity and provides clinically relevant information related to the progression and severity of an autoimmune inflammatory disease. The device does not achieve its principal intended action by pharmacological, immunological, or metabolic means, but through algorithmic analysis of medical images. Therefore, EpiFlare is qualified as a medical device under Regulation (EU) 2017/745.

### 1.5 Risk Class and Justification

Class IIa. EpiFlare is a software-based medical device (Software as a Medical Device, SaMD). Its classification has been determined in accordance with Regulation (EU) 2017/745 (EU MDR), Annex VIII, Rule 11. EpiFlare is software intended to provide information that is used to take decisions with therapeutic purposes. The information provided by EpiFlare does not have an impact that may cause death, irreversible or serious deterioration of health, or require surgical intervention; therefore, the device is classified as Class IIa.

In addition, when applying the IMDRF SaMD risk framework (IMDRF/SaMD WG/N12FINAL:2014), EpiFlare is considered to drive clinical management, as the information generated by the software is used to support treatment decisions and to identify early signs of disease flare. The target healthcare condition CDD is not considered critical, as accurate or timely treatment is not vital to avoid death or permanent disability. However, the condition may be considered serious, as timely treatment is needed to prevent long-term consequences (e.g. persistent symptoms or skin scarring). According to MDCG 2019-11, software that drives clinical management for serious conditions falls under Class IIa.

## 2. Intended Purpose

### 2.1 Intended Medical Purpose and Users

EpiFlare is intended to be used by qualified healthcare professionals, including dermatologists and primary care physicians, to support the monitoring of disease activity in adult patients with a confirmed diagnosis of CDD. Patients also interact with the system to capture and upload images under the supervision and responsibility of a healthcare professional.

### 2.2 Intended Patient Population and Medical Condition

- Adult patients (>=18 years) with a confirmed diagnosis of CDD.
- The device is intended for disease monitoring and assessment of flare severity; it is not intended to establish an initial diagnosis.

### 2.3 Clinical Setting

- Outpatient and community care settings (e.g. dermatology clinics, primary care practices).
- Home use by patients for image capture and upload, with subsequent review by a healthcare professional.

### 2.4 Geographic Region

Intended for use in the European Union. Additional regions may be added subject to regulatory approval.

### 2.5 Indications, Contraindications, Warnings, and Limitations

**Indications:** Monitoring of disease activity and detection of potential disease flares in patients with CDD.

**Contraindications:**

- Not intended for use in patients without a confirmed diagnosis of CDD
- Not intended for use by patients without supervision and responsibility of a qualified healthcare professional
- Not intended for use in pediatric populations (under age 18)

**Warnings and limitations:**

- EpiFlare does not provide a diagnosis and must not be used as the sole basis for treatment decisions.
- Image quality is essential for reliable outputs.
- Results must be interpreted by a qualified healthcare professional in the context of the patient's overall clinical presentation.

## 3. Overall Description

EpiFlare is a software-based medical device designed to support longitudinal monitoring of inflammatory skin disease activity in patients with a diagnosis of CDD. The system analyses skin images captured using a smartphone. Based on image analysis, the software generates the IDM that reflects disease activity or flare severity.

### 3.1 Principles of Operation and Mode of Action

The device operates by applying image processing and machine learning algorithms to smartphone skin images. Key visual features associated with inflammatory activity are extracted and analysed. These features are combined to produce the IDM, which is recorded in the patient record. When serial IDM measurement increases exceed two standard deviations or if the IDM exceeds an absolute value of >99, an alert is pushed to the healthcare provider. Additional, patient-specific alert thresholds can be set by the healthcare provider.

### 3.2 Novel Features

- Home-based image acquisition using a smartphone.
- Automated, standardised quantification of disease activity from images.
- Longitudinal tracking of disease trends to support early identification of flares.

### 3.3 Performance Characteristics (High-Level)

- Ability to analyse images of predefined quality.
- Generation of a reproducible quantitative output correlated with clinical disease activity.

## 4. Device Components and Features

### 4.1 Components

**Mobile application (patient-facing):**
- Used for image capture and secure upload.
- Provides guidance to the patient on correct image acquisition.

**Clinical application / web interface (clinician-facing):**
- Records IDM to patient record and visualises longitudinal trends.
- Flags results exceeding predefined thresholds.

**Backend processing module:**
- Performs image analysis and calculation of IDM.
- Manages data storage and security.

### 4.2 Component Relationships

Images captured via the mobile application are transmitted securely to the backend processing module. The analysis results are then made available to clinicians through the clinical interface.

### 4.3 Device Configurations

EpiFlare is supplied as a software-only solution consisting of the mobile application, backend services, and clinician interface. No alternative configurations are intended for the market.

## 6. Performance Specifications

The performance of EpiFlare is defined in terms of its ability to accurately and reliably quantify disease activity (IDM value) and to identify patients at increased risk of disease flare in the context of CDD. Performance specifications are aligned with the intended purpose and are supported by analytical and clinical performance evaluations.

### 6.1 Clinical Benefit

The expected clinical benefits of EpiFlare include:

- Accurate detection of elevated IDM values associated with increased disease activity in patients with CDD.
- Support identification of patients at higher risk of flare, enabling timely clinical review and potential treatment adjustment.

### 6.2 Measurement Range

The IDM value is reported on a continuous scale from 0 to 300.

### 6.3 Operating and Use Conditions

- Performance specifications apply to smartphone images captured using the specified camera resolution.
- Image quality requirements and acceptable acquisition conditions are defined in the Instructions for Use.

## 7. Previous and Similar Generations of the Device

### 7.1 Previous Generations

EpiFlare version 1.x: Earlier version providing image capture and basic qualitative assessment without the current quantitative IDM measurement and flare flagging functionality. This was a development prototype used for research purposes and was not available on the market.

### 7.2 Similar Devices on the Market

Other dermatology image analysis and disease monitoring software solutions intended for inflammatory skin conditions are available on the market. These devices generally provide image documentation and trend visualization but may differ in the level of automation and quantitative analysis provided by EpiFlare.
